## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Conservative (Tier 1)
**Biophotonic emission from neural tissue is a thermodynamic byproduct of synchronized metabolic activity, measurably correlated with electrophysiological state.**

The mechanistic pathway is clear: neural activity → mitochondrial ATP production → electron transport chain → reactive oxygen species (ROS) → photon emission via excited singlet oxygen and other luminescent species. This is not speculative — it is a known consequence of oxidative metabolism in any high-metabolic tissue, and the brain is the highest per-gram metabolic consumer in the body.

The Walker evidence establishes that deep sleep is a period of distinctive neural synchrony (characterized by high-amplitude, low-frequency delta oscillations). These synchronized states represent coordinated metabolic activity across large neural populations. The hypothesis predicts that slow-wave sleep would show a different biophotonic emission signature than REM or waking states — not because photons are doing anything special, but because the metabolic coordination is different.

Pharmacological disruption of sleep signatures (via GABAergic agents) would be predicted to alter this emission pattern accordingly. This is falsifiable with current technology: ultra-sensitive photomultiplier tubes positioned at the skull during polysomnography.

Ilya Prigogine's Nobel Prize-winning work on dissipative structures described how open systems far from thermodynamic equilibrium can spontaneously organize into higher-order patterns — but only by continuously processing energy from their environment and exporting entropy. The classic chemical example is the Belousov-Zhabotinsky reaction; the classic biological example is the living cell.

The nervous system meets every criterion for a dissipative structure:
1. It is thermodynamically open (requires continuous glucose/oxygen input)
2. It is far from equilibrium (maintains steep ion gradients across membranes at enormous energetic cost)
3. It exhibits spontaneous spatiotemporal organization (neural oscillations, coherent activity patterns)
4. Its organized states are maintained by energy throughput, not by equilibrium forces
