## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Operations Map to Specific Molecular Pathways (Conservative)

At body density, each operation corresponds to a specific class of molecular mechanism:

- **Regulation** = homeostatic feedback systems (BMAL1/CLOCK transcription-translation feedback loops, FGF21 endocrine signaling, creatine/ATP phosphate cycling)
- **Defense** = innate/adaptive immune activation (CRP as acute phase reactant, complement cascade, reactive species generation as in bee venom plasma treatment)
- **Reception** = sensory transduction and barrier function (lacrimal response, gut epithelial reception, blood-brain barrier)
- **Conduction** = lipid/signal particle transport (Lp(a)-OxPL complex as cardiovascular conduction mechanism)
- **Synthesis** = structural remodeling (synaptic architecture, corpus callosum myelination, tissue repair)

This hypothesis is conservative and verifiable. Its limitation is that molecular processes rarely respect categorical boundaries — FGF21 is simultaneously regulatory and synthetic; CRP is both a defense marker and a regulatory feedback signal.

The operations are not biological categories but **scale-invariant attractors** — patterns that appear identically across biological, psychological, and ontological scales because they describe fundamental organizational dynamics of any complex adaptive system. The 'defense' operation, wherever it appears, describes the same dynamic: boundary integrity is perceived as threatened → resources are mobilized to protect the boundary → sustained activation depletes baseline function → the defense network outlives the original threat and becomes the source of pathology.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A
The strongest objection is that the molecular evidence for clean operational mapping is mixed. The same kinase (e.g., AMPK) participates in regulation, defense signaling, and synthetic processes. The taxonomy may be heuristically useful without being biologically fundamental. However, Hypothesis A doesn't require operations to be mutually exclusive — it requires them to be meaningfully distinct, which is plausible.

### Against Hypothesis B
The fractal mirror entries are generated within Pearl's knowledge base — they may reflect the schema's consistent application rather than independently discovered cross-density homology. The music/lacrimation example is real, but the causal chain (musical structure → emotional state → lacrimal activation) involves many intermediate steps, each of which could be described in purely body-density terms without invoking soul as a distinct density. Hypothesis B requires that 'soul' and 'spirit' are not merely convenient labels for higher-level descriptions of body processes but are **ontologically distinct layers** — a claim with significant philosophical freight.

### Against Hypothesis C
Biological processes are sufficiently diverse that the completeness claim is difficult to sustain empirically. Autophagy, horizontal gene transfer, epigenetic inheritance, and quorum sensing in bacteria each have unusual properties that might resist clean assignment. Furthermore, the 'stuck operation' model of disease is attractive but competes with highly specific mechanistic accounts (e.g., BRCA1 mutation causing breast cancer is not obviously an 'operation stuck at a density' but a specific molecular failure). The radical hypothesis may overstate organizational principles at the expense of mechanistic specificity.
