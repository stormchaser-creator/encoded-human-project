### What Is Missing

The research focus explicitly names four bodies of work that are absent from the evidence set:

This extends the bioelectric framework beyond morphogenesis to behavior and potentially to collective biology.

- **Becker & Selden (1985, The Body Electric)**: The perineural DC system as a primary regulatory layer for tissue healing, pain modulation, and possibly consciousness. Becker proposed that the glial cells surrounding peripheral nerves constitute a DC electrical signaling network that is phylogenetically ancient and functionally distinct from the action-potential-based nervous system.

- **Zhao et al. This is among the strongest Tier 1 evidence for bioelectric signaling as a functional tissue regulatory mechanism.

- **McCaig et al. DC wound field reviews**: Comprehensive synthesis of applied and endogenous electric field effects on wound healing, establishing the clinical and mechanistic literature base.

The absence of these entries means the em_fields lens has no evidence nodes to activate. It is dormant not because bioelectric fields are irrelevant but because the relevant literature has not been ingested.

**Analytical Lenses**: em_fields (primary), topology_morphogenesis, control_theory, signal_processing.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection is inferential distance: Zhao et al.'s corneal wound healing findings involve local gradients across a few millimeters of epithelial tissue. Extrapolating to systemic pathology (allostatic load, MASH, OA, fibrosis) requires multiple inferential leaps, each of which could fail. The HPA chemical cascade is so well characterized — with decades of mechanistic research, validated biomarkers, and therapeutic interventions — that positing a bioelectric layer as causally primary requires evidence of temporal precedence. This evidence is largely absent from the ingested literature.

However, the observation that ECM reorganization precedes inflammatory pathology (decoded-60149b103db7) creates a genuine opening: if ECM conductance changes accompany or precede the biochemical cascade, that would be consistent with bioelectric regulation as an early-stage mechanism. This is testable.

### Against Hypothesis B

The most serious objection is scale mismatch. Tissue-level DC gradients operate across millimeters; organism-level emotional and autonomic states involve distributed systems spanning the entire body. There is no established transduction mechanism by which a disruption in local perineural DC signaling produces the global attractor-state disruption associated with trauma. Becker's perineural DC hypothesis, while generative, was never validated at the mechanistic level he proposed, and the field has largely moved toward chemical and action-potential-based models of CNS regulation.

The counter: HRV as a macroscale bioelectric index of vagal tone is already a validated clinical tool. The question is whether this index captures a subset of a broader bioelectric regulatory system, or whether it is the only bioelectric parameter that matters at the organism scale.

### Against Hypothesis C

This hypothesis risks category error: mapping a physical framework (bioelectric fields) onto a metaphysical concept ('The Kingdom') conflates domains in ways that Levin himself does not endorse. The claim that EMF pollution disrupts collective bioelectric fields is speculative and potentially non-falsifiable in its current form. This hypothesis should be held very lightly and investigated primarily through examination of whether Levin's framework genuinely supports supraorganismal extension, not through direct claims about contemplative categories.
