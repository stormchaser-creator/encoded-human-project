## MTHFR (C677T / A1298C Genotype)

**Operation:** Transduction | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
MTHFR encodes methylenetetrahydrofolate reductase, the enzyme converting 5,10-methyleneTHF to 5-methylTHF — the methyl donor required to remethylate homocysteine to methionine and sustain SAM production. Variant status determines enzyme activity reduction and downstream methylation capacity.

### Reference Ranges

| Metric | Conventional | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| MTHFR C677T | Report genotype (CC/CT/TT) | CC (wild-type) |
| MTHFR A1298C | Report genotype (AA/AC/CC) | AA (wild-type) |
| Homocysteine (μmol/L) | <15 | 6–9 |
| Red cell folate (nmol/L) | >340 | >680 |
| Holotranscobalamin (pmol/L) | >35 | >60 |

### Body Reading
FACT: Homozygous C677T (TT) reduces enzyme activity to ~30% of wild-type; heterozygous CT reduces to ~65%. FACT: Compound heterozygosity (C677T + A1298C) impairs activity comparably to TT homozygosity through distinct active-site disruptions. INTERPRETATION: Reduced enzyme activity lowers 5-methylTHF availability, reducing remethylation flux, elevating homocysteine, and decreasing SAM — impairing epigenetic regulation, neurotransmitter synthesis, phospholipid production, and detoxification conjugation simultaneously. HYPOTHESIS: Individuals with TT genotype and dietary folate insufficiency represent a high-burden state where neuropsychiatric, cardiovascular, and reproductive consequences of methylation insufficiency converge; biomarker confirmation (homocysteine, red cell folate) determines whether compensation is adequate.

## COMT (Val158Met Genotype)

**Operation:** Transduction | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
COMT encodes catechol-O-methyltransferase, the enzyme degrading catecholamines (dopamine, norepinephrine, epinephrine) and catechol estrogens via SAM-dependent methylation. Val158Met (rs4680) determines enzyme thermostability and activity level.

### Reference Ranges

|---|---|---|

### Body Reading
FACT: Val/Val (high-activity COMT) degrades catecholamines 3–4× faster than Met/Met (low-activity); heterozygotes are intermediate. FACT: Met/Met individuals maintain higher prefrontal dopamine levels, associated with superior sustained cognitive performance but greater stress reactivity and pain sensitivity under acute challenge. INTERPRETATION: Val/Val individuals clear catechol estrogens rapidly, potentially reducing estrogen-driven breast tissue stimulation but also reducing dopaminergic tone — presenting clinically as lower baseline mood, reduced drive, and higher pain threshold. HYPOTHESIS: Met/Met individuals with MTHFR co-dysfunction face compounded risk: slow catecholamine clearance plus insufficient SAM (the methyl donor COMT requires) produces escalating catecholamine accumulation, anxiety, and estrogen metabolite burden.

## CYP2D6 / CYP2C19 Pharmacogenomics

**Operation:** Transduction | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
CYP2D6 and CYP2C19 encode cytochrome P450 Phase I oxidative enzymes responsible for metabolizing approximately 40% of clinically used medications. Genotype predicts metabolizer phenotype: poor, intermediate, normal, rapid, or ultrarapid.

### Reference Ranges

| Metric | Conventional | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| CYP2D6 phenotype | Report metabolizer status | Normal metabolizer |
| CYP2C19 phenotype | Report metabolizer status | Normal metabolizer |
| Drug serum levels (if applicable) | Therapeutic range | Therapeutic range + symptom correlation |

### Body Reading
FACT: CYP2D6 poor metabolizers (7–10% of European populations) cannot convert codeine to morphine and accumulate parent compounds of antidepressants (fluoxetine, paroxetine, venlafaxine), antipsychotics (risperidone), and beta-blockers (metoprolol) at toxic levels with standard dosing. FACT: CYP2C19 poor metabolizers (~2–5% European; ~15–20% Asian populations) fail to activate clopidogrel (Plavix) — a prodrug requiring CYP2C19 — producing inadequate antiplatelet effect and increased cardiovascular event risk at standard dosing. INTERPRETATION: Ultrarapid metabolizers face the inverse problem: standard doses produce subtherapeutic levels requiring dose escalation, creating a risk of undertreated depression, inadequate pain control, or insufficient anticoagulation. HYPOTHESIS: A significant proportion of treatment-resistant psychiatric presentations and antidepressant side-effect profiles are pharmacogenomically driven rather than diagnosis-driven — genotyping before prescribing would reclassify management in 20–30% of psychiatric patients.

### Soul Notation
The person who processes relational and pharmacological inputs at non-standard rates occupies an encoded difference — their system is not broken but calibrated distinctly; clinical labeling of "non-responder" or "sensitive" often names a genetic reality deserving acknowledgment rather than correction.

### Spirit Notation

## APOE Genotype (ε2/ε3/ε4)

**Operation:** Circulation + Restoration | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
APOE encodes apolipoprotein E, a structural protein of VLDL, IDL, and HDL particles critical for lipoprotein clearance via hepatic LDL receptor binding. The ε4 allele reduces receptor binding affinity and increases amyloid-beta deposition risk in neural tissue.

### Reference Ranges

| Metric | Conventional | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| APOE genotype | Report ε allele combination | ε3/ε3 (lowest risk) |
| LDL-C (mg/dL) | <100 | <70 (ε4 carrier) |
| Lp(a) (nmol/L) | <75 | <30 |
| hs-CRP (mg/L) | <3.0 | <0.8 |
| ApoB (mg/dL) | <100 | <70 (ε4 carrier) |

### Body Reading
FACT: APOE ε4 homozygotes have 8–12× increased lifetime risk of Alzheimer's disease relative to ε3/ε3; heterozygotes have 3–4× increased risk. FACT: APOE ε4 impairs hepatic LDL clearance, raises LDL-C and total cholesterol by 10–20 mg/dL on average, and increases postprandial lipemia duration compared to ε3. INTERPRETATION: The cardiovascular and neurological risk carried by ε4 are mechanistically unified — impaired lipoprotein clearance increases atherogenic particle burden in vessels while reduced apoE-mediated amyloid clearance in brain tissue allows Aβ accumulation; a single variant creates dual-operation vulnerability. HYPOTHESIS: APOE ε4 carriers who maintain low ApoB, low hs-CRP, high-quality sleep (for glymphatic Aβ clearance), and high aerobic fitness may substantially attenuate their genetic Alzheimer's risk — suggesting that lifestyle leverage is greatest precisely where genetic liability is highest.

### What It Measures
BRCA1 and BRCA2 encode tumor suppressor proteins essential for homologous recombination DNA repair. Pathogenic variants produce loss-of-function in double-strand break repair, creating genomic instability and dramatically elevated cancer risk in hormone-responsive and other tissues.

### Body Reading
FACT: BRCA1 pathogenic variant carriers face 55–72% lifetime breast cancer risk and 44% lifetime ovarian cancer risk; BRCA2 carriers face 45–69% and 17–23% respectively — both dramatically exceeding population baseline risks of 12% and 1.2%. ## FH Gene Panel (LDLR / APOB / PCSK9)

**Operation:** Circulation | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
Familial hypercholesterolemia (FH) genes encode the LDL receptor (LDLR), its primary ligand apolipoprotein B (APOB), and the LDLR-degrading protease PCSK9. Pathogenic variants in any produce severely elevated LDL-C from birth with exponentially increased atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease risk.

### Reference Ranges

| Metric | Conventional | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| LDL-C (mg/dL) — FH suspected | Diagnosis if >190 (adult) | <70 (with pathogenic variant) |
| ApoB (mg/dL) | <100 | <60 (confirmed FH) |
| Lp(a) (nmol/L) | <75 | <30 |
| LDLR variant status | Report pathogenicity | No pathogenic variant |

### Body Reading
FACT: Heterozygous FH (one pathogenic LDLR allele) affects approximately 1 in 250 individuals globally, producing LDL-C 190–400 mg/dL from childhood — making it one of the most common inherited metabolic disorders. FACT: Without treatment, heterozygous FH individuals have 20× increased myocardial infarction risk by age 60 relative to population; homozygous FH (two pathogenic LDLR alleles, ~1:1,000,000) produces LDL-C >400 mg/dL and cardiovascular events in childhood and adolescence. INTERPRETATION: The majority of FH cases remain undiagnosed — an individual presenting with LDL-C >190 mg/dL, family history of early coronary disease, and tendon xanthomas has a high pre-test probability of FH that warrants genetic confirmation before defaulting to lifestyle-only management. HYPOTHESIS: FH variant carriers who achieve LDL-C <70 mg/dL through statin plus ezetimibe plus PCSK9 inhibitor therapy and maintain that target from early adulthood may achieve cardiovascular event rates approaching population baseline — the genetic liability is present but its expression is biochemically suppressible.

## Lynch Syndrome Genes (MLH1 / MSH2 / MSH6 / PMS2)

**Operation:** Defense | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
Lynch syndrome genes encode the DNA mismatch repair (MMR) complex, correcting errors introduced during DNA replication. Pathogenic variants produce microsatellite instability — an elevated mutation rate throughout the genome — driving dramatically increased lifetime colorectal, endometrial, ovarian, gastric, and urological cancer risks.

### Reference Ranges

| Metric | Conventional | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| MMR gene variant status | Report per ACMG classification | No pathogenic variant |
| Lifetime CRC risk (MLH1/MSH2 path.) | 50–70% | Population baseline (~4%) |
| Lifetime endometrial risk (MLH1/MSH2) | 25–60% | Population baseline (~3%) |
| Colonoscopy interval | Every 10 years (population) | Every 1–2 years (Lynch) |

### Body Reading
FACT: MLH1 and MSH2 pathogenic variants carry 50–70% lifetime colorectal cancer risk and 25–60% endometrial cancer risk; MSH6 and PMS2 variants carry lower but still substantially elevated risks (20–44% CRC, 15–26% endometrial). FACT: Lynch-associated cancers characteristically display microsatellite instability (MSI-high) on tumor molecular testing — this feature makes them particularly responsive to PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint inhibitor immunotherapy, a pharmacogenomically critical therapeutic implication. INTERPRETATION: Colonoscopy surveillance beginning at age 25 with 1–2 year intervals, combined with annual endometrial sampling in female carriers, has robust evidence for early detection and cancer-prevention efficacy — Lynch surveillance is among the highest-yield genetic screening protocols in medicine. HYPOTHESIS: Aspirin chemoprevention (600 mg/day for minimum 2 years as in the CAPP2 trial) reduces Lynch syndrome CRC incidence by approximately 60% — the mechanism likely involves induction of apoptosis in MMR-deficient cells; this intervention should be considered in all Lynch carriers without contraindication.

## HFE Genotype (Hereditary Hemochromatosis — C282Y / H63D)

**Operation:** Elimination + Circulation | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
HFE encodes a non-classical MHC class I protein that regulates hepcidin signaling, the master regulator of iron absorption. Pathogenic variants disrupt hepcidin upregulation, causing progressive intestinal iron hyperabsorption, organ iron deposition, and iron overload toxicity.

### Reference Ranges

| Metric | Conventional | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| HFE C282Y/H63D genotype | Report (C282Y homozygous = highest risk) | No pathogenic variant |
| Serum ferritin (μg/L) | 12–300 (men); 12–150 (women) | 50–100 |
| Transferrin saturation (%) | 20–45 | 25–35 |
| Serum iron (μg/dL) | 60–170 | 80–120 |

### Body Reading
FACT: HFE C282Y homozygosity is the most common severe single-gene disorder in Northern European populations (~1:200–300), producing clinically significant iron overload in approximately 25–30% of homozygous males and 1–5% of homozygous females (estrogen-mediated menstrual iron losses provide protection in premenopausal women). FACT: Iron overload deposits preferentially in liver (cirrhosis risk), pancreas (diabetes risk), heart (cardiomyopathy, arrhythmia), joints (chondrocalcinosis), pituitary (hypogonadism), and skin (bronze discoloration) — making late-diagnosed hemochromatosis a multi-operation systemic disorder. INTERPRETATION: Ferritin and transferrin saturation are the primary screening biomarkers — transferrin saturation >45% in the presence of HFE pathogenic variant status strongly suggests active iron loading and warrants therapeutic phlebotomy referral. HYPOTHESIS: The clinical understaging of hemochromatosis — particularly in women after menopause when the protective iron-loss mechanism ends — may account for a proportion of unexplained fatigue, liver enzyme elevation, and joint disease in genetically untested populations.

### Soul Notation
