## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A
Hypothesis A is empirically solid but incomplete. The original query's use of 'interpretation' and 'framework' implies a meta-level analytical structure, not merely a description of photobiology. If the query were only about photobiology, it would not have generated 'missing densities' of soul and spirit. The photobiological pipeline is a necessary but insufficient answer.

### For Hypothesis A
The mechanistic evidence is direct and multiply sourced. The singlet oxygen pathway is established biochemistry. The control light environment protocol is clinically actionable. This is the hypothesis least likely to mislead.

### Against Hypothesis B
The fractal extension relies on mirror entries that are synthesized rather than primary. 'Receptor array' as applied to attachment history is metaphor. The risk is that a structurally elegant metaphor is mistaken for a mechanistic truth, leading to category errors in clinical application.

### For Hypothesis B
The convergent structure of independently generated mirror entries is striking. The BOLT score, signal transduction, and time-restricted eating mirrors each arrive independently at the same operational logic. This cross-entry convergence is evidence that the framework has generative power beyond any single entry.

### Against Hypothesis C
Biophoton research remains contested. The leap from 'light affects biology via known photochemical mechanisms' to 'organisms communicate via coherent biophoton fields' is significant. The spirit-density mirror entry, while evocative, cannot serve as evidence for electromagnetic consciousness theories.

### For Hypothesis C
The specific emphasis on 'non-native' vs. 'native' EMF in the Jack Kruse entry is unexplained by simple photochemistry. If frequency-specificity beyond blue-light photochemistry is operationally significant, coherence-based mechanisms become more plausible. The specificity of the clinical claim is itself a weak but real signal.
