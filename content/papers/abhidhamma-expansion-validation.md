## 4. Unit-by-Unit C3/Tier Assessment (Flagged Issues Only)

### D-016: Chanda vs. Tanha (Pearl: Tier A) — DOWNGRADE TO TIER B

**Problem:** C3 is generous. The chanda/tanha distinction maps onto existing neuroscience of wanting vs. goal-directed action. Berridge's wanting/liking dissociation, plus the extensive literature on dopaminergic vs. non-dopaminergic motor pathways, already covers much of this prediction. The claim that "non-craving action-intention can generate behavior through a distinct neural pathway" is partially retrospective.

**Counterargument:** The specific claim that ALL volitional action can operate without ANY reward-anticipation circuitry is stronger than Berridge's dissociation (which is about different aspects of reward, not about reward-free action). Some neuroscientists argue all motivation is ultimately dopaminergic. This is a genuine debate.

**Verdict:** The prediction has value but is not clearly *beyond* existing neuroscience. **Tier B** — strong mapping, partially retrospective C3.

### D-020: Two Equanimities (Pearl: Tier A) — CONFIRMED TIER A

Genuinely non-trivial. Contemplative neuroscience treats equanimity as a single construct. The prediction of hedonic-equanimity vs. relational-equanimity neural dissociation under one experiential label has NOT been tested. Clean C3, clean C4.

### D-021: Samadhi Four Features (Pearl: Tier A) — HOLD TIER A WITH FLAG

**Problem:** Three of the four features (non-wandering, eliminating distraction, non-disturbance) are essentially the same thing described three ways. The conjunction prediction is inflated. Only the **hedonic component** (pleasure as obligatory proximate cause of deep concentration) is a genuinely non-trivial prediction.

**Verdict:** Hold Tier A based on the hedonic-obligatory prediction alone, which IS non-trivial — it predicts that deep concentration is incompatible with neutral or negative hedonic tone, contradicting views of concentration as affect-neutral. But flag the redundancy: the four features should be reduced to two genuinely dissociable predictions (attentional stability + obligatory hedonic tone).

### D-022: Four Satipatthana (Pearl: Tier A) — CONFIRMED TIER A

Strong four-way dissociation prediction. Current mindfulness neuroscience treats mindfulness as unitary or at best FA/OM. The prediction that body, vedana, citta, and dhamma mindfulness produce four dissociable neuroplastic outcomes is specific, testable, and non-trivial.

### D-023: Five Hindrances (Pearl: Tier A) — DOWNGRADE TO TIER B

**Problem:** C3 is generous. The five-way neural dissociation prediction boils down to "five different emotional states have five different neural profiles." This is almost trivially true. ANY five emotional states would have distinct neural signatures. The prediction doesn't demonstrate that the *Abhidhamma's specific five* add anything beyond what affective neuroscience already knows.

**What would save C3:** A specific prediction about how each hindrance interferes with absorption-specific neural dynamics (e.g., "sensual desire specifically disrupts the gamma-coherence substrate in a way distinguishable from how doubt disrupts it"). The scoring notes gesture at this but the actual C3 prediction doesn't operationalize it.

**Verdict:** Tier B. The taxonomy is coherent and the five-way claim is testable, but C3 doesn't exceed existing affective neuroscience.

### D-024: Seven Bojjhanga Balance (Pearl: Tier A) — DOWNGRADE TO TIER B

**Problem:** The U-shaped optimal function curve between energizing and calming factors is essentially the **Yerkes-Dodson law** (1908). This has been known for over a century. The prediction that "neither maximum arousal nor maximum calm produces optimal performance" is one of the most well-established findings in psychology/neuroscience.

**What would save C3:** The specific prediction about sati (mindfulness) as a meta-regulatory factor that operates across both poles is more interesting, but it's only one element of the claimed prediction.

**Verdict:** Tier B. Genuine mapping but C3 is retrospective — Yerkes-Dodson already predicts the inverted-U.

### D-026: Effort-Based Elimination Logic (Pearl: Tier A) — DOWNGRADE TO TIER B; FLAG AS REDUNDANT

**Problem:** This unit is a restatement of D-003 with an explanatory mechanism added. D-003 already covers the invariant sequential elimination of jhana factors. D-026 adds "the sequence follows effort-ordering" — but this is an interpretation of WHY D-003 works, not a new empirical claim.

**Additional concern:** If both D-003 and D-026 count as Tier A, the same empirical prediction (ordered elimination) is being double-counted. The metabolic-cost-ordering prediction IS interesting but it's testing the same underlying claim.

**Verdict:** Tier B at best if retained as a separate unit. Consider merging with D-003 as a sub-prediction or removing entirely to avoid double-counting.

### D-028: Mind-Door vs. Sense-Door (Pearl: Tier A) — DOWNGRADE TO TIER B

**Problem:** C3 is generous. The distinction between perception (sensory) and conception/imagination (mental) is one of the most well-established findings in cognitive neuroscience. Hippocampal future simulation, prefrontal abstract thought, and the distinction from primary sensory processing are thoroughly documented.

**The scoring notes even acknowledge this:** "While this is broadly consistent with neuroscience's distinction between perception and conception..." — then tries to save C3 with the claim about "doorways" vs. "contents." But the specific falsification test offered ("disrupting sensory processing should not impair mental-door processing") is trivially true. People can think while blindfolded. This is not a non-trivial prediction.

**Verdict:** Tier B. Strong mapping but C3 adds nothing beyond what cognitive neuroscience has known for decades.

## 7. Red Flags

### Flag 1: C3 Generosity Creep (CRITICAL)
The expansion batch shows a systematic pattern of C3 inflation. In the original 15, Pearl's C3 assessments were well-calibrated — even the two marginal cases (D-001, D-006) were identified by the validator as having genuine non-trivial elements. In the expansion batch, at least 5 units have C3 scores that accept established neuroscience as novel prediction.

**Root cause hypothesis:** Pearl may have been generating expansion units to reach the target of 33 (matching Patanjali's count) and relaxed C3 thresholds to maintain a high Tier A rate.

### Flag 2: Redundancy (D-026 duplicates D-003)
D-026 (effort-based elimination logic) is an interpretation of D-003 (invariant sequential elimination), not a separate claim. Including both inflates the Tier A count by double-counting.

### Flag 3: No Directionality Violations (POSITIVE)
Despite C3 generosity, the directionality safeguard remains fully intact. No neurobiological terms contaminate any claim statement. This is the most critical check and it passes cleanly.

### Flag 4: Source Verification Improved
The expansion batch avoids the Visuddhimagga bleed problem found in the original 15 (D-001, D-003, D-009). All expansion units cite Sangaha chapters correctly. Improvement noted.

### Flag 5: Tier B/C/D Classifications Are Accurate
Pearl's lower-tier classifications (B, C, D) in the expansion batch are all defensible. The calibration drift is isolated to the A/B boundary.
