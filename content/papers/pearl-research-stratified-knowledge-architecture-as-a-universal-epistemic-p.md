### Evidence Cluster 2: The Trade-Off Between Isolation and Coherence

The mitophagy entry (GRK-elimination) provides an unexpected but structurally important insight. Drp1-mediated mitochondrial fission segregates damaged domains from healthy mitochondrial networks — this is domain isolation. The mechanism is beneficial for selective quality control: PINK1 recruits Parkin specifically to the damaged subdomain, enabling targeted degradation.

However — and this is crucial — **inhibiting Drp1 (preventing fission) actually increases overall mitophagy flux and Parkin recruitment across the whole network**. Domain isolation, taken too far, suppresses system-wide quality control. The cell must balance domain segregation (for selective accuracy) against network connectivity (for systemic throughput).

This is a precise biological model of the tension in knowledge base architecture: **high domain specificity increases precision within a domain but may reduce the system's ability to answer cross-domain questions**. A knowledge base so finely stratified that no bridges exist between domains will fail at exactly the kind of query Pearl received — a meta-architectural question that sits at the intersection of information science, epistemology, and system design.

### Hypothesis C: Phase Transition Dynamics (Tier 3 — Speculative)

Knowledge base domain expansion is a phase transition phenomenon. Below a critical density threshold in any domain, the system behaves as a disordered high-entropy state with low coherent retrieval. **Lenses**: Phase transitions, Chaos attractors, Entropy, Complexity emergence

**Falsifiable by**: Monotonically linear improvement in retrieval quality with corpus expansion (no inflection point) would contradict phase transition dynamics.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The analogy between GWAS stratification and knowledge base architecture may not hold because the mechanisms differ fundamentally. Genomic stratification reduces statistical confounding from hidden population structure. Knowledge base retrieval confounders are semantic — ambiguous query terms, polysemous concepts, context-dependent relevance. Domain tags in a knowledge base operate more like metadata filters than like genuine statistical stratification. The information-theoretic gains may not transfer.

However, the empirical record from FECD phenotyping and sex-stratified PD is robust: stratification consistently reveals hidden structure. Even if the mechanism differs, the functional outcome (better signal recovery) may generalize.

### Against Hypothesis B

Siegel's framework was developed for human developmental psychology, not knowledge engineering. Applying 'embodied-relational' dimensions to software architecture involves a category error that could produce misleading design intuitions. The mitochondrial fission analogy similarly risks anthropomorphizing a cellular mechanism.

Counterpoint: Pearl's architecture explicitly uses body/mind/soul/spirit as organizational categories derived from integrative human experience frameworks. This architectural choice commits the system to the logic of those frameworks. If Siegel's framework describes how those dimensions interact in human cognition, it is architecturally relevant to a system designed around those same dimensions.

### Against Hypothesis C

Phase transition language is frequently applied metaphorically to complex systems without empirical grounding. There is no validated order parameter for 'knowledge base coherence,' no identified control parameter, and no empirical evidence that Pearl's soul/spirit gap specifically represents subcritical dynamics rather than simple underpopulation. The hypothesis may be unfalsifiable in practice.

Counterpoint: The categorical failure mode Pearl demonstrates — returning irrelevant evidence rather than degraded but relevant evidence — is qualitatively distinct from graded performance degradation and is consistent with subcritical behavior. This is at least consistent with, if not proof of, phase transition dynamics.

## Synthesis

The three hypotheses are not mutually exclusive. They describe different aspects of the same architectural challenge:

1. **Hypothesis A** describes the value of stratification (why to do it)
2. **Hypothesis B** describes the failure mode of over-isolation (what goes wrong without bridges)
3. **Hypothesis C** describes the non-linear dynamics of corpus growth (how the system transitions between states)

The evolved insight integrates all three: **effective domain-stratified corpus expansion requires distinguishing between density operations (adding entries within existing strata) and topological operations (adding new relationship types and bridge structures between strata)**.
