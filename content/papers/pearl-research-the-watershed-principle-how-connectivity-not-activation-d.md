# The Watershed Principle: Connectivity, Not Activation, as the Locus of Interoceptive Awareness

## Abstract

Two research targets — Farb et al. (2013) on fronto-insular connectivity in mindfulness, and Mehling (2018) on MAIA-2 factor structure — share a common methodological and conceptual challenge: the most meaningful signal lies not in individual variables or activation peaks, but in the relational topology between measurement layers. This document synthesizes available evidence to generate testable hypotheses about the connectivity analysis approach in Farb 2013, the factor-differentiated criterion validity of MAIA-2 subscales, and the unifying principle that detection and regulatory modulation of interoceptive signals are functionally and neurally distinct processes that should not be aggregated. Three competing hypotheses are evaluated, and a medium-confidence evolved insight is proposed: fronto-insular resting-state connectivity will correlate specifically with MAIA's regulatory/appraisal subscales but not with the Noticing subscale, because resting connectivity indexes the modulation layer, not the detection layer.

### The MAIA-2 Gap

The Multidimensional Assessment of Interoceptive Awareness (MAIA), originally published by Mehling et al. in 2012, contains eight subscales: Noticing, Not-Distracting, Not-Worrying, Attention Regulation, Emotional Awareness, Self-Regulation, Body Listening, and Trusting. The MAIA-2 update (2018) was motivated in part by factor instability identified in confirmatory analyses — particularly in the appraisal-adjacent subscales.

The research question asks whether experiential/appraisal subscales (Not-Worrying, Trusting, Body Listening, possibly Self-Regulation) load *differently* from receptive subscales (Noticing, Not-Distracting) on meditation experience variables. This is a theoretically crucial question because:

- If all subscales load equivalently, MAIA total score is a defensible aggregate measure
- If they dissociate, aggregation destroys signal and potentially obscures which component of 'interoceptive awareness' is being trained by different meditation practices

## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Methodological-Conservative

Farb 2013 used seed-based functional connectivity with the anterior insula as primary seed, consistent with prevailing methodological conventions. Connectivity changes, if reported, were computed on task-residualized or resting-state BOLD signal and are therefore genuinely independent of activation by construction. No MAIA correlation was reported because the instrument was either not administered or had not yet been validated for this application.

**Analytical lenses**: Network theory (hub identification), information theory (signal separation from noise/activation), signal processing (filtering task-evoked response before analyzing connectivity structure).

**Falsifiable by**: Full text showing ICA or DCM methods; or showing activation and connectivity are reported from the same unresidualized signal; or showing MAIA was administered and correlated.

### Hypothesis B: Integrative-Control-Theory

MAIA's subscales tap two functionally distinct interoceptive layers: a bottom-up detection layer (Noticing, early Not-Distracting) and a top-down regulatory/appraisal modulation layer (Not-Worrying, Body Listening, Trusting, Self-Regulation). Meditation training differentially strengthens the regulatory layer's capacity to modulate the detection layer — which is exactly what fronto-insular connectivity (PFC→insula feedback) versus insula activation (detection) would measure independently. MAIA-2's factor revision likely separated these two layers more cleanly, and criterion validity correlations with meditation experience variables should show the regulatory subscales differentiating meditators from non-meditators more than the Noticing subscale.

**Analytical lenses**: Control theory (feedforward detection vs. feedback modulation; gain control; setpoint regulation), signal processing (filter selectivity by experience level), fractals (cell-level mechanosensing/migration distinction fractalizes to neural detection/regulation distinction).

**Falsifiable by**: MAIA-2 CFA showing all subscales on single factor; or meditation experience correlating equally with Noticing and regulatory subscales; or Farb 2013 showing only DMN suppression with no fronto-insular connectivity component.

### Hypothesis C: Topological-Radical

The research gap itself — Pearl's inability to retrieve Farb 2013 and Mehling 2018 — is structurally homologous to the scientific gap those papers address. In both cases, the most important information exists in the relational space between variables, not in any single variable, and is therefore invisible to any analysis that treats activation and connectivity as equivalent, or that aggregates MAIA subscales. This is not merely a poetic observation: information-theoretically, aggregation across functionally distinct subsystems destroys the differential signal. The MAIA total score and 'brain activation during interoception' are both examples of collapsed dimensionality that obscure the topology of interoceptive processing.

**Analytical lenses**: Topology/morphogenesis (the shape of the problem determines what is visible), information theory (aggregation as signal destruction), complexity/emergence (the meaningful property emerges from the relationship, not the elements).

**Falsifiable by**: Activation fully accounting for group differences in Farb 2013; or MAIA total score predicting outcomes as well as subscale-differentiated models.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The primary weakness is its circularity: 'most likely used seed-based connectivity because that was the norm' does not constitute evidence. PPI analysis was also common in this era and would allow connectivity to be computed as a function of task context — a different meaning of 'connectivity change independent of activation.' If Farb used PPI rather than resting-state FC, the separation from activation is conceptually different and potentially weaker. Additionally, the 2013 paper may have used a pre-MAIA interoceptive awareness measure that is not directly comparable to current MAIA subscales, making criterion validity comparisons anachronistic.

### Against Hypothesis B

The fractal mapping from integrin-biology to psychological measurement is analytically elegant but epistemically thin. Biological systems regularly instantiate functional distinctions that do not correspond to the distinctions embedded in a questionnaire designed by human researchers. The MAIA-2 revision might have separated factors for psychometric reasons (item redundancy, floor/ceiling effects) rather than because the two-layer detection/regulation model was confirmed. Furthermore, the control-theory framework predicts differential loading, but many other theoretical frameworks (e.g., common-factor interoception) would predict the opposite.

### Against Hypothesis C

The homology between information access failure and measurement instrument limitation is a category error dressed in topological language. The fact that both involve 'invisible relational information' does not mean they share causal structure. The poetic resonance of the watershed metaphor can mislead by suggesting the gap is somehow necessary or meaningful rather than simply an access problem that would be resolved by reading the papers.

### Strongest Convergence

All three hypotheses converge on one empirical prediction that is independently testable: **resting-state fronto-insular connectivity should correlate more strongly with MAIA regulatory/appraisal subscales than with the Noticing subscale in a meditator-enriched sample.** This prediction is specific, falsifiable, theoretically grounded in control theory, biologically analogous in ECM-integrin systems, and phenomenologically described in the sauna protocol mirrors. It does not require the Farb 2013 or MAIA-2 full texts to be valid — it is a forward prediction that existing researchers could test.

## Synthesis

The evolved insight from this analysis is that interoceptive awareness is a cascade system with at least two empirically separable layers: (1) signal detection (Noticing; insula activation; mechanosensing in biological tissue) and (2) regulatory modulation of that detection (Not-Worrying, Trusting, Body Listening; fronto-insular connectivity; EMT-mediated coordination in tissue). The clinical and contemplative significance of meditation training may reside primarily in the second layer — not in sharpening detection (though this may occur) but in transforming the appraisal and regulatory relationship to detected signals.

This would explain why:
- Fronto-insular CONNECTIVITY (not activation) is the more meaningful outcome measure in Farb 2013
- MAIA's regulatory subscales (not Noticing) are the ones that differentiate experienced meditators from novices
- Body-based trauma therapies (Bessel van der Kolk's group work; somatic experiencing) work not by enhancing detection but by changing the regulatory response to detected signals

## Implications

### For Measurement

If MAIA's regulatory subscales are the ones that track meditation experience and neural connectivity change, then studies using MAIA total score are likely underestimating the effect of meditation on interoceptive regulation while potentially overestimating effects on raw detection. Subscale-differentiated analysis should be standard.

### For Intervention Design

If the regulatory layer is the target, then meditation practices that explicitly cultivate equanimous observation (open monitoring, noting practice, body scan with non-reactive awareness) should produce stronger MAIA regulatory subscale gains and stronger fronto-insular connectivity changes than practices that primarily sharpen detection (breath focus without non-reactivity training).

### For Neuroscience

The prediction that fronto-insular resting-state connectivity correlates with appraisal/regulatory MAIA subscales but not Noticing would, if confirmed, provide a neural mechanism for why 'interoceptive accuracy' (measured by heartbeat detection tasks) and 'interoceptive awareness' (measured by MAIA) dissociate in many studies. They are measuring different layers of the same cascade.

### For the Fractal Model

The bile duct leading-tip cell mechanism suggests that in biological tissue, the 'regulatory layer' (EMT-partial migration-executing cells) must maintain partial identity with the detection layer (integrin-alpha2-high mechanosensing) — it cannot fully differentiate. The therapeutic analog would be: effective interoceptive regulation requires maintained contact with the detection layer (continued Noticing) while the appraisal response to Noticing transforms. Over-differentiation (complete dissociation from body signals) and under-differentiation (no regulatory capacity above detection) are both pathological.
