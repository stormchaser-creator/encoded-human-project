### Hypothesis B: The Nrf2-Homology Hypothesis (Tier 2 — Integrative)

**Claim:** Peer review functions not as direct error correction but as a catalytic-regulatory intervention that restores the author's own upregulatory capacity — their ability to detect and neutralize conceptual errors. **The Nrf2 architecture:**

The sulforaphane entry establishes that LPS-induced depression in mice is reversed not by administering an antidepressant but by administering sulforaphane, which activates Nrf2, the master regulator of the cell's own antioxidant response. - The 'depressive collapse' is the paper's failure to reach its own argumentative potential
- 'Directly attacking' = editors rewriting the paper for the author
- 'Sulforaphane-like intervention' = specific, targeted reviewer comments that activate the author's own critical capacity

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection to the control theory framework is that biological control systems have been shaped by millions of years of survival-selective pressure, while academic review systems have only centuries of iteration without equivalent selection pressure for truth-detection. Journals that publish false positives can survive and thrive; organisms that misregulate blood glucose do not. The structural analogy may be valid but the selection dynamics are entirely different, meaning the system may have control-theory architecture without control-theory function.

The strongest support is that control theory predictions are scale-independent precisely because they derive from the mathematics of feedback, not from the specific substrate. The prediction that systems with high gain and insufficient damping will oscillate — endless revision without convergence — is observable in real editorial processes and matches the mathematical prediction regardless of whether the system was selected for truth.

### Against Hypothesis B

The Nrf2 homology assumes the author is the primary locus of epistemic defense. In large collaborative papers, in highly standardized methodological fields, or in cases of genuine fraud, the 'author's own upregulatory capacity' may be insufficient or irrelevant. The homology may overweight individual epistemic agency at the expense of systemic and institutional factors.

The strongest support is that the distinction between catalytic-regulatory and substitutive interventions is empirically detectable: studies of writing pedagogy consistently show that directive feedback (telling students what to write) produces lower long-term development than facilitative feedback (asking questions that activate the student's own critical capacity). The Nrf2 pattern appears to hold across educational contexts at a functional level.

### Against Hypothesis C

The spirit-gap framing risks projecting a teleological framework onto a domain that operates — legitimately — on different values: rigor, reproducibility, incremental verification, error-correction. What looks like suppression of futurity-signaling may be appropriate epistemic caution, the necessary immune response of a knowledge system to the constant pressure of wishful thinking, motivated reasoning, and paradigm capture.

The strongest support is that the reproducibility crisis is real, documented, and severe across multiple fields. The system is not merely cautious — it is actively filtering out null results, replication failures, and paradigm-challenging findings in a way that does not optimize for truth but for a particular aesthetics of confident positive results. The 'caution' defense does not account for the documented systematic biases in the direction of suppression.

## Synthesis: The Three-Density Revision Workflow

The three hypotheses are not mutually exclusive. They describe the same system at different densities:

**Body density (Hypothesis A):** The revision workflow is a regulatory feedback system. It can be optimized by improving setpoint clarity (explicit acceptance criteria), reducing lag (faster review turnarounds), calibrating gain (matching the specificity of reviewer demands to the actual epistemic distance between submission and publishable threshold), and adding appropriate damping (editorial buffer between reviewer comments and author response).

**Soul density (Hypothesis B):** The revision workflow is an Nrf2-like restoration process. It works best when it rebuilds the author's own upregulatory capacity rather than substituting for it. This means reviewer comments should be Socratic rather than prescriptive, mentorship in revision should prioritize narrative coherence over technical compliance, and the felt sense of a successful revision should be revelation rather than relief.

**Spirit density (Hypothesis C, partially):** The revision workflow should function as a growth-pole activator — restoring the field's collective orientation toward epistemic futurity. Currently, it is not functioning at this density. The system optimizes for local regulatory compliance at the cost of the global growth signal. Restoring spirit-density function would require structural changes: protection of null results, incentives for paradigm-disrupting submissions, recognition that the 'never felt better' signal — the surplus of aliveness that indicates genuine growth-pole activation — is a valid epistemic indicator, not a confound to be controlled.

## Implications

### For Individual Researchers

Understanding revision as a three-density process changes the phenomenology of receiving reviewer comments. Body-level: what is the specific regulatory gap the reviewer identified, and what is the minimum intervention that closes it? Soul-level: is this comment activating my own critical capacity, or am I treating it as a prescription to comply with? Spirit-level: does this revision bring the paper closer to its orientation toward the question it is actually trying to answer, or does it move it toward a version of the question that is easier to defend?

### For Journals and Editors

The Nrf2 homology suggests that editorial processes should be evaluated not only by their output quality (is the published paper better?) but by their developmental quality (is the author better equipped to write the next paper?). Journals that function as sulforaphane — activating authors' own upregulatory capacity — produce different long-term epistemic outcomes than journals that function as direct correctives.

### For Research Culture

The spirit-density gap is the most urgent implication. If consciousness has a growth-pole, and if that growth-pole can be inhibited without the system knowing it has been inhibited, then the current state of academic publishing — characterized by the 'never felt better' suppression described in the growth hormone entry — requires not optimization but restoration. The question is not how to make the current system run better, but what minimal intervention (the sulforaphane dose, the growth hormone signal) would restore the field's own capacity to detect and correct its epistemic errors.
