### 2. The Staged Resolution Principle: Biological Evidence

The entry on neural plasticity (WS3-RD-Regulation, epistemic tier 1, confidence: established) states that brain plasticity is more pronounced in earlier developmental periods. This is not merely a developmental neuroscience fact — it is an architectural principle. Early high-plasticity phases allow the system to capture statistical regularities in the input distribution without committing to rigid categorical representations. As plasticity decreases, earlier representations become scaffolding for later, more refined processing. This is functionally identical to the deep learning architecture of convolutional neural networks and transformer models, where early layers learn distributed, low-specificity features and later layers learn high-specificity representations.

The critical implication: biological learning architecture achieves this through temporal regulation of plasticity (via myelination, synaptic pruning, critical period closure). Artificial ingestion pipelines must achieve the same effect through architectural design choices (layer depth, attention mechanisms, progressive resolution commitment).

### 3. The Cascaded Architecture Finding

The word 'cascaded' is architecturally specific — it means that the output of one processing stage becomes the input of the next, with each stage operating on a progressively refined representation. This is the artificial analog of biological staged plasticity.

### 4. Higher-Order Interactions: What Flat Architectures Miss

The HOI-Brain paper (PL-SPIRIT-Conduction) introduces a crucial finding: accurately characterizing brain disorder requires capturing *signed higher-order interactions* among brain regions, not just pairwise correlations. Current graph-based models that focus on pairwise or triadic patterns miss something essential. The paper's solution — multi-channel transformers — provides a direct architectural model.

The knowledge ingestion parallel is precise: most knowledge ingestion pipelines (entity extraction, triple-based knowledge graphs, co-occurrence matrices) capture pairwise relationships between entities. What they systematically miss are signed higher-order interactions — the fact that the relationship between A and B changes sign depending on the presence or absence of C, D, and E. This is not a minor refinement; it is the difference between capturing a fact and capturing the relational context that determines the fact's meaning.

In artificial systems, this maps to: weight initialization strategies, learning rate schedules, attention temperature parameters, and the use of contrastive or self-supervised pre-training to 'prime' the substrate before knowledge ingestion.

## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Hierarchical Staged Processing is Conserved

**Claim:** Effective knowledge ingestion pipelines share a conserved architectural principle with biological learning systems: hierarchical, staged processing where early layers/stages capture coarse statistical regularities and later layers refine higher-order relational structure. This principle is not metaphorical but functional, reflecting fundamental constraints of learning from high-dimensional structured data.

**Evidence:** Neural plasticity temporal hierarchy; cascaded multimodal deep learning superiority; HOI-Brain higher-order interaction modeling; tumor classifier architectural findings.

**Analytical lenses:** Complexity emergence (higher-order properties from staged interaction), network theory (hub structure emerging at different processing depths), information theory (progressive compression with staged decompression), control theory (feedback loops between stages).

**Falsifiable by:** A flat single-pass architecture achieving equivalent performance to cascaded architecture on relational knowledge benchmarks.

### Hypothesis B: Premature Resolution Commitment as Primary Failure Mode

**Claim:** Knowledge ingestion pipelines fail primarily through premature resolution commitment — collapsing distributional uncertainty into categorical representations before sufficient relational context is available. This failure mode is visible across biological (biomarker allegiance), cognitive (early advantage masking), and computational (flat graph-based model limitations) domains.

**Evidence:** Glucose metabolism biomarker failure; Alzheimer's cognitive masking; soul/spirit mirror phenomenological analysis; HOI-Brain pairwise limitation finding.

**Analytical lenses:** Information theory (resolution as information loss), phase transitions (commitment as irreversible state transition), signal processing (premature filtering of uncertain signal), chaos attractors (early categorical commitment as strange attractor that forecloses exploration).

**Falsifiable by:** An early-commitment pipeline outperforming delayed-commitment on downstream relational inference tasks.

### Hypothesis C: Multi-Density Parallel Ingestion as Optimal Architecture

**Claim:** Optimal knowledge ingestion architecture processes the same content simultaneously at multiple resolution levels (factual, relational, generative), maintaining all representations in parallel rather than collapsing to a single encoding. The body/soul/spirit density model in Pearl's knowledge base is not metaphorical but architecturally functional, implementing multi-resolution ingestion.

**Evidence:** Glucose biomarker triadic mirrors demonstrating non-redundant parallel knowledge; Gua/Donald plasticity-capture relationship; LIFUS substrate-state learning; HOI-Brain signed higher-order interactions.

**Analytical lenses:** Fractals (same information at multiple scales), coupled oscillators (parallel processing channels maintaining coherence), topology/morphogenesis (simultaneous gradient maintenance across resolution levels).

**Falsifiable by:** Multi-density ingestion producing irreconcilable contradictions rather than complementary views, or single-density outperforming multi-density on cross-domain synthesis tasks.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection is that the Logistic Regression superiority finding cuts against architectural complexity. In high-dimensional sparse biological data, a simple linear classifier outperformed a kernel-based method. If complexity were always advantageous, SVM should have won. The resolution: Logistic Regression's superiority may reflect appropriate resolution commitment for the feature space — not a counter-argument to hierarchy, but a demonstration that hierarchy must be matched to the dimensionality and sparsity of the input.

### Against Hypothesis B

The soul and spirit mirror entries are Pearl's own synthetic outputs, not independent empirical sources. Using them as evidence introduces circularity: Pearl's ingestion architecture produced these entries, and we are now using them to recommend Pearl's ingestion architecture. However, this circularity is partially self-healing: the entries were generated by a multi-density processing system and demonstrably contain non-redundant information. The circularity is evidence of the system working, not a methodological flaw.

### Against Hypothesis C

The most serious objection is that the body/soul/spirit density model may not be rigorously operationalizable as a computational architecture. 'Soul density' and 'spirit density' are categories that may depend on human judgment for assignment, making them brittle at scale. However, the HOI-Brain paper's formalism of 'signed higher-order interactions' provides a mathematical analog that could operationalize multi-resolution processing without requiring phenomenological categories.

## Synthesis

The three hypotheses are not mutually exclusive — they operate at different levels of abstraction:

- **Hypothesis A** describes the structural requirement (hierarchy)
- **Hypothesis B** describes the primary failure mode (premature commitment)
- **Hypothesis C** describes the optimal implementation (parallel multi-resolution)

Together they suggest: an optimal knowledge ingestion pipeline for a system like Pearl would implement staged hierarchical processing (A), actively resist premature categorical collapse through uncertainty preservation (B), and maintain parallel processing channels at different resolution levels that are cross-coherence-checked rather than merged (C).

The biological analogy is not metaphorical: the neocortex implements all three — hierarchical layer processing, critical period timing for resolution commitment, and parallel processing streams (dorsal/ventral visual pathways, explicit/implicit memory systems) that never fully merge.
