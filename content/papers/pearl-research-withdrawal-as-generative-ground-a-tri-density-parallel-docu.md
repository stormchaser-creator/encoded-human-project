## Evidence Review

### Body Density — Tier 1 Anchor

**Source:** Rachel Yehuda (WS3-RY), epistemic Tier 1, confidence: high

The strongest body-density evidence in this evidence set is Yehuda's finding that FKBP5 methylation was lower in Holocaust offspring compared to controls. FKBP5 is a co-chaperone of the glucocorticoid receptor — its methylation state modulates how sensitively cells respond to cortisol. Lower methylation in offspring means the stress-response system is epigenetically primed toward hyperreactivity, even in individuals who did not themselves experience the original trauma.

This is the paradigmatic case of what might be called **inherited regulatory setpoint displacement**: the body receives not just genetic information from its ancestors but epigenetic annotation — a kind of molecular memory that adjusts physiological thresholds before the individual has any experience of their own. The mechanism is not metaphorical. It is measurable at the base-pair level.

Structural note: This finding has been subject to methodological scrutiny. Yehuda conducted replication with internal resources after grant denial, which limits the independence of the replication. Effect size is not reported in the available entry. These limitations are noted but do not undermine the Tier 1 classification, as the finding has been cited in peer-reviewed literature and represents the best available empirical anchor for transgenerational epigenetic inheritance in humans.

**Supporting evidence:** The lateral habenula dysfunction entry (Sapolsky, WS2-RS, Tier 1) provides convergent support. The lateral habenula is described as exhibiting 'screwy' activity in depression — it is an anti-reward center that, when dysregulated, generates persistent negative affect independent of environmental input. This is precisely what inherited epigenetic dysregulation would produce: a regulatory circuit whose setpoint has been displaced, generating signal (negative affect, stress reactivity) that is no longer proportionate to stimulus.

**Analytical lenses:** control_theory (setpoint displacement, feedback gain), information_theory (compressed trauma signal transmitted across generations), network_theory (FKBP5 as a hub gene in the HPA axis network).
