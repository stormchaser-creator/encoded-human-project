### Preamble: Defense and Restoration as Paired Operations

Each number is a timestamp on a biological negotiation between entropy and renewal. The physician's task is not to treat the number but to decode what the body is reporting about the state of that negotiation.

HYPOTHESIS: Chronic prostatic inflammation driven by gut dysbiosis or metabolic syndrome may sustain low-grade PSA elevation below the cancer-referral threshold while seeding malignant transformation over years.

## AFP (Alpha-Fetoprotein)

**Operation:** Defense | **Body Codx**

### What It Measures
Fetal serum protein normally suppressed post-birth; re-expressed by hepatocellular carcinoma and germ cell tumors (testicular, ovarian); hepatic injury without malignancy produces mild transient elevation.

### Reference Ranges

| Metric | Conventional | Functional Optimal |
|---|---|---|
| AFP (adult, non-pregnant) | < 10 ng/mL | < 5 ng/mL |
| Mild elevation | 10–20 ng/mL | Recheck; hepatic injury possible |
| Cancer-concern threshold | > 20 ng/mL | Image immediately |
| High burden | > 400 ng/mL | HCC or germ cell tumor very likely |

### Body Reading
FACT: AFP > 400 ng/mL in a cirrhotic patient is diagnostic of hepatocellular carcinoma with > 95% specificity per AASLD criteria — imaging confirmation, not biopsy, is sufficient for treatment initiation. INTERPRETATION: Mild AFP elevation (10–30 ng/mL) in a non-cirrhotic individual most likely reflects acute hepatocellular injury (viral hepatitis, alcoholic hepatitis, drug-induced) rather than malignancy; liver function tests and viral serologies contextualize the signal. INTERPRETATION: In men under 40 with elevated AFP and no liver disease, testicular cancer must be excluded before any other interpretation — this is the most curable solid tumor in men and delay is the primary preventable harm. HYPOTHESIS: Serial low-level AFP rise (crossing from < 5 to 10–20 ng/mL over 12–24 months) in cirrhotic patients may represent early HCC below ultrasound resolution — multiphasic MRI is the appropriate next step rather than watchful waiting.

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Detectable cfDNA at baseline | Tumor confirmed circulating; level roughly proportional to burden |
| Declining cfDNA on therapy | Active treatment response; continued monitoring every 4–8 weeks |
| Rising cfDNA on therapy | Resistance emerging; therapy reassessment urgently indicated |
| Undetectable cfDNA post-treatment | Complete molecular response; surveillance q3–6 months |
| Re-emergent cfDNA after undetectable | Recurrence detected; typically 3–6 months before imaging visibility |

### Body Reading

INTERPRETATION: A rising cfDNA variant allele frequency (VAF) during therapy — even from 0.1% to 0.3% — is a biologically meaningful resistance signal that should prompt imaging and consideration of therapy adjustment before clinical deterioration; do not wait for imaging confirmation when molecular signal is unambiguous. INTERPRETATION: Undetectable cfDNA at end of treatment does not equal cure but defines a molecular remission state with measurably lower recurrence risk; serial surveillance catches re-emergence at the most therapeutically actionable moment. HYPOTHESIS: Emerging resistance mutations detected in cfDNA before clinical progression provide a therapeutic window — the interval between molecular resistance signal and clinical failure — that represents the highest-value moment for therapy modification, potentially extending progression-free survival by months.
