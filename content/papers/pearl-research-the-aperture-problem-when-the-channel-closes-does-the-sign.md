### 1.2 ERV Biology: What IS Peer-Reviewed

The following represents established ERV science (from literature external to Pearl's evidence set, noted for transparency):

- **Chuong et al. (2016, Science):** ERV-derived sequences function as regulatory enhancers for interferon-stimulated immune genes. This is the canonical demonstration that ERVs serve real regulatory functions in the human genome. **Tier 1.**
- **Bourque et al. (2018, Nature Reviews Genetics):** Review of transposable element contributions to regulatory networks across mammals. **Tier 1.**
- **Sundaram & Wysocka (2020, eLife):** ERV-derived enhancers show tissue-specific activity and can be activated by inflammatory signals. **Tier 1.**

None of these papers connect ERV sequences specifically to the MAO-A locus.

### 1.3 MAO-A Promoter Biology: What IS Peer-Reviewed

Monoamine oxidase A (MAO-A) is located on the X chromosome (Xp11.3) and encodes the primary enzymatic degrader of serotonin, norepinephrine, and dopamine in the synaptic cleft. Its expression is regulated by:

- **CpG island promoter methylation:** Hypermethylation suppresses, hypomethylation increases transcription. Meyer et al. (2006), Philibert et al. (2008). **Tier 1 external.**
- **VNTR (variable number tandem repeat) in the promoter region:** 3.5 and 4 repeat alleles drive higher transcription than 3-repeat alleles. Sabol et al. (1998). **Tier 1 external.**
- **SP1 and other transcription factors:** Canonical regulation through binding sites in the proximal promoter.

The critical gap: no published ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, or reporter assay data specifically demonstrates ERV-derived regulatory elements influencing MAO-A transcription. This gap is the target for future investigation.

The hypothesis is conservative but not fully grounded within the current evidence set. ### For Hypothesis A
Of the three hypotheses, A is the most buildable from peer-reviewed components. The SAM/SAH ratio as a master regulator of methylation-dependent gene expression is established biology. MAO-A's CpG island promoter is documented. Yehuda's transgenerational data provides the developmental frame. This is the backbone that should structure any soul entry draft.

### Against Hypothesis B
The cross-modal remapping analogy is intellectually seductive but epistemically risky. Cortical remapping operates over developmental timescales through synaptic plasticity mechanisms (Hebbian learning, pruning) that have no direct molecular parallel in VNTR-methylation dynamics. Calling them 'analogous' may create false explanatory satisfaction.

### For Hypothesis B
The MAO-A VNTR is one of the most replicated gene-behavior associations in psychiatry (with appropriate caveats about replication crises). The interaction between VNTR and early adversity in predicting adult behavior (Caspi et al. 2002 — the foundational gene × environment interaction study) is exactly the kind of two-channel regulatory logic Hypothesis B proposes. This is real and documented.

### Against Hypothesis C
Zach Bush is not a peer-reviewed source on ERVs. This must be stated plainly. Building scientific hypotheses on podcast claims — even from a credentialed physician — violates Pearl's epistemic standards. The ERV-MAO-A connection may exist, but asserting it without published evidence, even at Tier 3, risks normalizing the use of non-peer-reviewed claims as hypothesis anchors.

### For Hypothesis C
The underlying biology is genuine. ERVs DO regulate genes. Microbiome metabolites DO alter epigenetics. MAO-A IS epigenetically regulated. The three-part chain is plausible. The hypothesis is worth submitting to ENCODE/literature search. It belongs in the 'worth investigating' category, clearly labeled as unanchored speculation with a defined path to evidence.
