# Metaphor Guide for Light Machine Anthology

Every section must include:

1. OPENING METAPHOR — Begin each section with a vivid metaphor that orients the reader to what this cluster of biomarkers is actually doing in the body. Examples:
   - Lipid panel: "Think of your cardiovascular system as a river delta — the particles are barges, the vessels are channels, and the conditions we're measuring tell us whether goods are moving or piling up at the dock."
   - Thyroid/HPA: "The stress-thyroid axis is like a thermostat and a fire alarm in the same room — when the alarm keeps firing, eventually the thermostat stops trusting its own readings."

2. OPERATION METAPHORS — For each operation named (Reception, Transduction, Conduction, Regulation, Synthesis, Defense, Restoration, Elimination), include a brief metaphor that makes the operational language concrete to a non-clinical reader.

3. DENSITY BRIDGES — When moving from body → soul → spirit reading of a biomarker, use a transition phrase that makes the shift feel natural, not jarring. Suggested templates:
   - "In the body, this looks like... In the life, this often shows up as... At the deepest level, this is asking..."
   - "The number is telling us X. The pattern is asking Y. The design question underneath is Z."

4. READER-FRIENDLY GLOSSARY INSERTS — For any clinical term that would lose a non-MD reader, include a one-sentence plain English parenthetical immediately after the term.

5. CLOSING RESONANCE — End each section with a paragraph that lands the clinical material in lived experience. Not a summary — a felt sense of what it means to have these markers, this pattern, this question.
