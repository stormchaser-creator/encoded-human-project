## What We Know From External Neuroscience Literature (Research Synthesis)

While not in Pearl's current KB, the following represents the consensus from recent high-impact literature that Pearl should ingest:

### NfL (Neurofilament Light Chain)

- NfL is a cytoskeletal protein found in the axons of neurons. When neurons are damaged or die, NfL is released into CSF and subsequently plasma.
- **It is sensitive but not specific to Alzheimer's.** NfL rises in multiple sclerosis, ALS, frontotemporal dementia, traumatic brain injury, and other neurodegenerative conditions.
- In Alzheimer's disease, plasma NfL:
  - Begins rising in the **preclinical phase**, sometimes 4-6 years before symptom onset
  - Correlates with cortical thinning and white matter changes
  - Distinguishes AD dementia from healthy controls with AUC ~0.85-0.93 depending on population
  - Does NOT reliably distinguish AD from other dementias with comparable neurodegeneration
- The MDD study finding of *stable* NfL confirms that neuronal loss is not a feature of depression, which is clinically useful.

### GFAP (Glial Fibrillary Acidic Protein)

- GFAP is an intermediate filament protein expressed primarily in astrocytes. Its release into blood reflects astrocytic activation and injury.
- **GFAP may be more Alzheimer's-specific than NfL**, as recent evidence suggests GFAP elevation tracks specifically with amyloid burden, even in preclinical stages.
- Plasma GFAP:
  - Is elevated in Alzheimer's patients with confirmed amyloid pathology
  - May precede symptom onset
  - Distinguishes Alzheimer's from frontotemporal dementia better than NfL alone
  - AUC for AD vs. controls: ~0.85-0.92 in recent plasma biomarker studies
- Critically, GFAP reactive astrogliosis is partially amyloid-driven, which explains why it may track amyloid burden more specifically than NfL.

### Combinatorial Panels

The most powerful diagnostic approach emerging from recent literature combines:
1. **Aβ42/Aβ40 ratio** (upstream amyloid imbalance)
2. **phospho-tau 217 (pTau217)** (tau pathology, highly Alzheimer's-specific)
3. **GFAP** (reactive astrogliosis, amyloid-correlated)
4. **NfL** (neurodegeneration burden, nonspecific)

The Alzheimer's Association's 2024 revised diagnostic criteria incorporate plasma biomarkers into the diagnostic framework for the first time, acknowledging that plasma Aβ42/Aβ40 and pTau217 have reached clinical-grade accuracy. NfL and GFAP serve as injury/staging markers rather than etiology markers.

## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Neurodegeneration Signal, Non-Alzheimer Specificity (Tier 1 — Conservative)

**Claim:** NfL and GFAP are sensitive neurodegeneration markers elevated in Alzheimer's disease, but their diagnostic utility is fundamentally limited by cross-disease elevation. Their primary value is to confirm that structural CNS damage is occurring (distinguishing neurodegeneration from psychiatric states like MDD), not to specify Alzheimer's as the etiology.

**Evidence anchor:** The MDD study (decoded-ec35e25cb8bd) directly demonstrates that NfL/GFAP are NOT elevated in a common neuropsychiatric condition, validating their ability to identify neurodegeneration as a class. However, this same logic implies they cannot distinguish between different neurodegenerative diseases.

**Analytical lenses:** Information theory (signal-to-noise ratio; NfL/GFAP carry neurodegeneration signal but compress disease-specific information), network theory (these are downstream hub readouts of multiple upstream failure modes).

**Falsifiable by:** Demonstration of Alzheimer's-specific NfL elevation profiles not seen in other dementias at equivalent neurodegeneration burden.

### Hypothesis B: Combinatorial Triangulation for Staging and Etiology (Tier 2 — Integrative)

**Claim:** NfL and GFAP gain diagnostic specificity for Alzheimer's when combined with amyloid and tau markers because they contribute orthogonal information: amyloid ratio captures upstream pathological driver, pTau captures disease-specific phosphorylation pathology, and NfL/GFAP capture the downstream injury cascade. Alone, they are weak classifiers; combined, they triangulate both etiology and stage.

**Evidence anchor:** The MDD study shows Aβ peptide changes occurring independently of NfL/GFAP stability — this orthogonality in MDD suggests that these markers tap different pathological dimensions. If they were perfectly correlated, they would all move together in any condition affecting the amyloid pathway.

**Analytical lenses:** Entropy (NfL = cytoskeletal entropy increase; GFAP = reactive phase transition), complexity emergence (diagnostic accuracy as emergent property of multi-marker interaction), control theory (amyloid/tau as setpoint disturbances; NfL/GFAP as downstream damage accumulation).

**Falsifiable by:** Multi-marker panel not significantly outperforming best single marker (likely pTau217) in prospective Alzheimer's diagnosis studies.

### Hypothesis C: Temporal Staging Hierarchy Across Disease Progression (Tier 3 — Speculative)

**Claim:** Different biomarkers cross clinically meaningful thresholds at different disease stages, creating a temporal hierarchy: Aβ42/Aβ40 ratio falls first (preclinical amyloid accumulation), GFAP rises second (reactive astrogliosis driven by early amyloid deposition), NfL rises third (axonal damage from established tau and amyloid pathology). Maximum diagnostic accuracy therefore requires knowing WHERE in the disease course the patient sits, and using the appropriate marker for that window.

**Evidence anchor:** The dissociation observed in MDD (Aβ changes while NfL/GFAP remain stable) is consistent with amyloid processing being altered earlier and more sensitively than structural damage markers. The phase transitions lens suggests each marker represents a different critical threshold in a multi-step degradation cascade.

**Analytical lenses:** Phase transitions, coupled oscillators (biomarkers as desynchronized oscillators that cross threshold at different times), chaos attractors (disease state as attractor, biomarker trajectories as paths toward it), topology morphogenesis (disease as gradient unfolding across molecular dimensions).

**Falsifiable by:** Cross-sectional evidence that NfL, GFAP, and Aβ42/Aβ40 all elevate/decline simultaneously from earliest detectable pathology, with no temporal precedence.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection to Hypothesis A is that it may understate GFAP's Alzheimer's specificity. Recent evidence suggests GFAP elevation correlates more strongly with amyloid burden than with tau burden or general neurodegeneration — implying GFAP may be partially amyloid-driven and therefore more Alzheimer's-specific than NfL. If GFAP can distinguish Alzheimer's from FTD (which lacks amyloid pathology) even at equivalent neurodegeneration burden, then the 'non-specific' framing is too broad. The MDD evidence remains supportive of A for the psychiatric/neurodegeneration distinction, but the specificity claim may be overstated for GFAP.

### Against Hypothesis B

The strongest objection is combinatorial panel complexity introducing practical clinical barriers. If pTau217 alone achieves AUC >0.95 for Alzheimer's diagnosis (as some recent studies suggest), adding NfL and GFAP may add minimal incremental accuracy while adding cost and complexity. The triangulation value of NfL/GFAP may be primarily for staging and prognosis (injury burden) rather than diagnosis per se. This doesn't falsify B but reframes the clinical utility question.

### Against Hypothesis C

Linear temporal staging assumes a relatively homogeneous disease course. Alzheimer's is heterogeneous — limbic-predominant, hippocampal-sparing, and posterior cortical variants follow different pathological trajectories. The temporal hierarchy may represent a population average that misleads in individual cases. Additionally, rapid-progressors may show simultaneous elevation of all markers, compressing the staging window to clinically undetectable resolution.

## Synthesis

The evolved insight integrates the strongest elements of all three hypotheses:

**NfL and GFAP are best understood as injury-burden markers that confirm neurodegeneration is occurring and quantify its magnitude, while the Aβ42/Aβ40 ratio and pTau217 provide etiological specificity for Alzheimer's.** In the diagnostic workflow:

1. **Screening layer:** Aβ42/Aβ40 ratio and pTau217 establish Alzheimer's etiology
2. **Staging layer:** NfL and GFAP quantify neurodegeneration severity and injury burden
3. **Differential layer:** GFAP may provide additional specificity over NfL due to amyloid-driven astrogliosis
4. **Exclusion layer:** Normal NfL/GFAP helps rule out neurodegeneration in patients presenting with cognitive or mood symptoms (as demonstrated in MDD)

The MDD study's finding that NfL/GFAP are stable while Aβ peptides shift represents a subtle but important signal: amyloid processing is perturbed in depression without structural neuronal damage. This has implications both for Alzheimer's risk in depressed patients and for the interpretation of amyloid biomarkers in neuropsychiatric populations.

## Implications

### For Alzheimer's Diagnosis

- A patient with elevated plasma GFAP + reduced Aβ42/Aβ40 + elevated pTau217 has strong multimodal evidence for Alzheimer's pathology
- A patient with elevated NfL + normal GFAP + normal amyloid markers should prompt investigation of non-Alzheimer's neurodegeneration
- A patient with normal NfL/GFAP + normal amyloid markers + cognitive symptoms should be evaluated for psychiatric/functional etiology
