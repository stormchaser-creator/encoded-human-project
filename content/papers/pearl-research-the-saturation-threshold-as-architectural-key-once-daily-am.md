## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: The Saturation Threshold Mechanism (Conservative, Tier 1 pending)

**Claim:** Once-daily aminoglycoside dosing reduces nephrotoxicity and ototoxicity specifically because renal proximal tubular cells and cochlear hair cells possess saturable uptake mechanisms (megalin/cubilin receptor-mediated endocytosis) that become occupied at peak concentrations but clear during the extended trough period — preventing lysosomal accumulation beyond the threshold that triggers autophagic cell death.

This is the canonical mechanistic explanation in the aminoglycoside literature (not yet retrieved but established pharmacological knowledge). The key features:
- Uptake is saturable: receptor occupancy follows Michaelis-Menten kinetics
- With ODA dosing: peak occupies receptors maximally, but trough (18-20 hours) allows receptor recycling and drug efflux
- With MDD dosing: receptors never fully clear; drug accumulates in lysosomes across doses; lysosomal phospholipidosis and membrane disruption occur
- The protective variable is not peak height alone (ODA peaks are HIGHER than MDD) nor total AUC (can be equivalent) but specifically the ratio of occupation time to clearance time — the duty cycle

**Analytical lenses:** Phase transitions (accumulation crosses a threshold triggering cell death), control theory (receptor occupancy as the regulated variable with a setpoint), signal processing (the trough as a filter that prevents DC accumulation), information theory (receptor saturation as loss of signal discrimination capacity).

**What would falsify it:** If pharmacological blockade of megalin receptors during MDD dosing prevented nephrotoxicity at equivalent AUC, this would confirm the receptor-mediated accumulation mechanism. Conversely, if ODA with shortened trough (6 hours) produced equivalent toxicity to MDD, the trough duration (not merely pulsatility) would be identified as the critical variable.
