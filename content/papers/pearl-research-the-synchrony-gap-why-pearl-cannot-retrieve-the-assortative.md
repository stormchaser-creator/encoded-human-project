### Adjacent Evidence with Potential Relevance

Despite the gap, three evidence pieces deserve careful attention for their structural relevance:

**mPFC entry (Walker source)**: The medial prefrontal cortex is identified as the principal region for generating slow-wave (deep) sleep AND as the primary initial target of beta-amyloid accumulation in Alzheimer's disease. This creates a vulnerability loop: the region most responsible for the brain's nightly clearing function is also the most susceptible to the toxic buildup that occurs when that function fails. What makes this relevant to the synchrony query is that the mPFC is also a central node in social cognition, self-other processing, and — in the developmental literature — caregiver-infant attunement. If mPFC architecture is partly shaped by prenatal hormonal environments and early attachment experiences, then couples selected on physical phenotype (which is also partly shaped by prenatal hormonal environments) might show correlated mPFC profiles — and therefore correlated social attunement capacity.

**McGilchrist embodied cognition entry**: The right hemisphere, in McGilchrist's account, does not merely process emotion — it holds the embodied subject as a living, felt whole. This is the processor of the very cues through which physiological compatibility might be unconsciously assessed: facial micro-expressions, postural coordination, breath rhythm, skin conductance changes visible in flushing or pallor. If assortative mating on physical traits is partially functioning as an autonomic compatibility screen, the right hemisphere is the neural substrate through which that screen operates.

**Fractal mirror entries (soul/spirit)**: These synthesis reflections describe how depletion at the soul and spirit density manifests as shortened relational patience, diminished narrative continuity, and loss of self-reflective capacity. These are not merely psychological metaphors — they map onto specific autonomic signatures: chronic sympathetic activation, reduced HRV, elevated cortisol baseline, impaired parasympathetic recovery. The observation that 'single-channel readouts of psychic depletion are real yet diagnostically insufficient' mirrors the methodological challenge in the physiological synchrony literature: individual autonomic measures are noisy; it is the coupling between two persons' autonomic time-series that carries the relational signal.

### Hypothesis B: Developmental Hormonal Environments Produce Correlated Phenotypic-Autonomic Profiles (Tier 2 — Integrative)

**Claim**: The hormonal environments that shape physical phenotype during fetal development and puberty (testosterone, estrogen, cortisol, growth hormone) also shape autonomic nervous system baseline and reactivity. Consequently, physical assortative mating may be implicitly selecting for autonomic compatibility — couples who resemble each other physically may start with more compatible autonomic operating parameters, facilitating faster and more stable physiological entrainment over time.

**Mechanistic pathway**:
- Prenatal testosterone → facial masculinization/feminization + ANS reactivity baseline
- Cortisol exposure → stress axis calibration + physical growth parameters
- Oxytocin system development → social engagement capacity + physical bonding cues
- mPFC architecture shaped by early hormonal and attachment environment → adult social attunement capacity

**Analytical lenses**: Topology/morphogenesis (same developmental gradients produce both form and function), coupled oscillators (compatible ANS baselines would entrain more readily), control theory (shared setpoints for autonomic regulation)

**Falsifiable by**: Studies showing physical similarity does not predict ANS baseline similarity in unacquainted individuals matched for age and social background

**Evidence support**: Indirect — mPFC entry (shared developmental vulnerability), McGilchrist (right hemisphere as embodied pattern processor), epigenetic inference (not directly evidenced in knowledge base)

**Confidence**: Low-medium. The pathway is biologically plausible but the specific evidence is absent.

### Hypothesis C: Physical Assortative Mating Is an Unconscious Autonomic Compatibility Screen (Tier 3 — Radical)

**Claim**: Humans assess physiological compatibility through proximal cues embedded in physical appearance, movement, and presence. The right hemisphere (McGilchrist) processes these cues below conscious awareness. Physical similarity serves as a phenotypic proxy for developmental and autonomic kinship. Sapolsky's claim that assortative mating occurs on physical traits is true but mechanistically underspecified: the deeper mechanism is that physical similarity is the most accessible readout of shared autonomic architecture, and mate selection is functioning as an autonomous compatibility scan.

**Analytical lenses**: Signal processing (physical appearance as compressed representation of developmental history), complexity emergence (relational stability as emergent property of compatible autonomic attractors), electromagnetic fields (biophoton and thermal emission as potential compatibility signals — highly speculative)

**Falsifiable by**: Showing that self-selected couples show no greater physiological synchrony at first meeting than randomly paired individuals; or that physical similarity does not predict ANS similarity in blind pairing studies

**Evidence support**: McGilchrist embodied cognition synthesis; fractal mirror spirit entry on consciousness weaving coherent selfhood across time (which stable autonomic co-regulation supports); structural analogy to Feldman maternal-infant synchrony (referenced but not evidenced)

**Confidence**: Low. This is a generative hypothesis for future research, not a defensible current claim.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The objection is not to its accuracy but to its utility. If Pearl's only output is 'the papers aren't here,' the research function has failed. The value of the Researcher role is to generate the strongest possible working hypotheses from available evidence while clearly marking their epistemic status. Hypothesis A is a necessary constraint, not a sufficient output.

### Against Hypothesis B

The hormonal pathway hypothesis conflates several distinct claims that each require independent support:
1. That prenatal hormones shape both physical phenotype AND ANS baseline (supported in the literature, but effect sizes vary)
2. That physical similarity between individuals implies similar prenatal hormonal environments (not well established)
3. That similar ANS baselines facilitate physiological entrainment in dyads (plausible, partially supported by resonance frequency HRV research, but not directly evidenced here)
4. That this developmental pathway is a meaningful contributor to assortative mating (entirely absent from evidence base)

Each step is individually plausible; their chain is speculative.

### Against Hypothesis C

This hypothesis risks being unfalsifiable in practice — if physical appearance encodes developmental history, and if that encoding is processed unconsciously, it becomes very difficult to design a clean test. The hypothesis also relies on the right hemisphere being a 'compatibility detector' in a way that McGilchrist's framework does not explicitly claim. McGilchrist describes the right hemisphere as the processor of embodied presence and living wholes — this is adjacent to, but not identical with, autonomic compatibility assessment.

The strongest version of the objection: social homogamy (matching on class, education, neighborhood, religion) can explain most of the variance in physical assortative mating through shared developmental environment alone, without invoking any autonomic compatibility mechanism.

## Implications

If Hypothesis B is correct (even partially), the implications for understanding relationship science are significant:

- Physical assortative mating would not merely be an aesthetic preference effect but a functional compatibility selection
- Couples who are physically dissimilar might face greater initial autonomic entrainment challenges, requiring more active co-regulation work
- The Levenson-Gottman finding that autonomic coupling predicts relationship stability might be partially a matching effect (couples who began with compatible autonomic profiles show stability because entrainment was easier) rather than purely a learned co-regulation effect
- Clinical implications: couples therapy focused only on behavioral co-regulation might be missing a biological substrate layer
