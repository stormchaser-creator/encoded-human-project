## The Core Pattern Being Investigated

Before generating hypotheses, it is useful to state precisely what the 'cross-density hysteresis pattern' means in the context of this investigation.

**Hysteresis** in dynamical systems refers to path-dependence: the state of a system depends not just on current inputs but on its history. Specifically, a system exhibits hysteresis when the threshold to enter a state differs from the threshold to exit it. A classic example: a spring requires 10 Newtons to compress but only 3 Newtons of spring force to return — the difference represents energy absorbed by the system.

In biological regulation, hysteresis appears when:
- Chronic stress requires substantially more intervention to resolve than it took to establish
- A traumatic memory, once encoded, requires a specific type of reactivation + mismatch signal to reconsolidate — it cannot be erased by simple extinction
- A neural network (like the DMN) reorganized by years of rumination does not simply revert when rumination stops

The 'cross-density' qualifier asks whether this pattern is visible at body, soul, AND spirit levels simultaneously, and whether these are independent manifestations of a single underlying principle or merely analogous patterns at different scales.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection is evidentiary: no direct HRV bifurcation studies are present in the knowledge base. The structural analogies drawn from proprioceptive loops and SCN dynamics are suggestive but not demonstrative. The MS compensation finding is a disease model that may not generalize to healthy autonomic regulation. Additionally, some HRV researchers argue that the 'loss of complexity' framework is overstated — that complexity reduction is adaptive in certain contexts, not always pathological.

However, the clinical literature on autonomic recovery after trauma, PTSD, and chronic stress is substantial. The asymmetric recovery dynamic (long to heal, fast to harm) is one of the most replicated findings in psychophysiology, even if the bifurcation formalism is rarely applied explicitly.

### Against Hypothesis B

The hearing aid/cognition null result is ambiguous. Effect size limitations, short follow-up (6-12 months), and measurement insensitivity in cognitive batteries could explain the null finding without invoking reconsolidation asymmetry. The hippocampus/chemo finding involves drug toxicity and may not generalize to psychological trauma. The inferential jump from 'restoration didn't work' to 'asymmetric reconsolidation threshold' requires additional mechanistic evidence.

However, Hypothesis B is the one with the strongest external corroboration. The reconsolidation literature is large and converges: Nader's boundary conditions studies, LeDoux's fear reconsolidation work, and the clinical literature on EMDR and Coherence Therapy all point toward asymmetric threshold dynamics that cannot be explained by simple extinction or re-exposure models.

### Against Hypothesis C

This is where honest epistemic accounting is most important. Hypothesis C compounds four inferential leaps simultaneously. The 'inversion' claim — that contemplative practice inverts the asymmetry direction so that exits become easier than entries — is the most speculative element and risks being unfalsifiable as stated. The concept of a 'single topological invariant' governing three different levels of description is mathematically plausible (via universality class arguments from statistical mechanics) but requires explicit formalization before it can generate testable predictions.

What remains genuinely striking is the structural recurrence: the proprioceptive loop appears at body, soul, and spirit density with the same internal logic and the same failure mode, without being forced. This is at minimum a useful heuristic model.
