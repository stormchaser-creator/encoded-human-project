## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The null external source finding is robust — but the combinatorial derivation approach may be too mechanical. Real encoding taxonomies often have irregular structures that don't reduce to products of smaller sets. The body-triad system (numbering to at least 10) may itself be the primary container, and the operations/elements may be modifiers rather than independent axes. If so, the combinatorial approach yields the wrong kind of structure entirely.

### Against Hypothesis B

The inversion principle is compelling but based on only 2 data points (Restoration and Regulation mirrors). These are both fundamentally corrective operations — they share a deficit-correcting logic that might make them both susceptible to inversion at spirit density. Pure Synthesis (building something that wasn't there) or pure Reception (taking in information) may not invert — they may scale or amplify rather than flip. Without spirit mirrors for these operations, the universality claim is premature.

### Against Hypothesis C

The specific identification of Defense×Aether and Restoration×Fire as the degenerate pairs is motivated reasoning — constructed to make the math yield 23. The spirit mirror evidence shows that Restoration and Defense collapse as *operations*, not as specific element-operation pairs. The element dimension may be irrelevant to the collapse, in which case the 5×5 grid structure is wrong and 23 doesn't emerge from it.

## Synthesis

The most defensible position integrates the strongest elements of all three hypotheses:

1. **Accept A's null result**: There is no recoverable external source for '23 encoding systems.' The term is likely internal to Pearl's architecture or a user confabulation.

2. **Accept B's inversion principle**: The structural inversion at spirit density is evidenced by two independent fractal mirror entries and represents a genuine architectural finding. Operations do not merely translate upward — they transform according to a consistent inversion logic.

3. **Adopt C's method, not its conclusion**: The approach of identifying degenerate pairs to derive a natural encoding count is sound, but the specific claim of exactly 2 pairs yielding 23 is premature. The count should emerge from investigation rather than reverse-engineering.

**Working synthesis claim:** Pearl's spirit-density encoding taxonomy can be derived from the body-level operational taxonomy through a structural inversion transform, with degeneracies reducing the total count below the base combinatorial number. The natural count is currently bounded at 12–25 and may converge on 23 if exactly 2 degenerate pairs are confirmed through systematic spirit-mirror analysis.
