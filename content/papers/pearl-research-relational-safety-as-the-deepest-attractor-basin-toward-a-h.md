# Relational Safety as the Deepest Attractor Basin: Toward a Hierarchical Nested-Setpoint Model of the Nervous System

## Abstract

This investigation triangulates three major theoretical frameworks — Stephen Porges's Polyvagal Theory, Karl Friston's Active Inference / Free Energy Principle as applied to trauma, and the allostasis literature of Bruce McEwen and Peter Sterling — to evaluate a specific structural claim: that the nervous system is organized as a *hierarchical attractor landscape* in which relational safety constitutes the deepest and oldest basin, with all other physiological setpoints nested within it. Drawing on available evidence from Porges's social engagement model, McGilchrist's hemispheric hierarchy, and dynamical systems frameworks for biological self-organization, three competing hypotheses are generated and debated. The evidence moderately supports the hierarchical model, with the strongest convergence occurring around the 'inside-out' regulatory principle, the phylogenetic ordering of autonomic states, and the clinical observation that relational safety is a *prerequisite* for higher-order regulatory flexibility rather than one option among equals. A significant evidence gap exists for the Friston / free-energy pillar. Confidence is rated **medium**: the structural argument is coherent and multiply supported, but direct empirical measurement of 'basin depth' in the required dynamical-systems sense remains underdeveloped.

## Evidence Review

### 1. Polyvagal Theory: Hierarchy as Phylogenetic Nesting

Stephen Porges's Polyvagal Theory proposes three hierarchically organized neural circuits corresponding to evolutionary stages of vertebrate development:

1. **Dorsal vagal complex** (unmyelinated vagus): the oldest circuit, shared with reptiles, mediating immobilization, shutdown, and freeze responses to life threat.
2. **Sympathetic nervous system**: the mobilization circuit, enabling fight/flight responses.
3. **Ventral vagal complex** (myelinated vagus, unique to mammals): mediating social engagement, co-regulation, play, and what Porges calls the 'safety bucket.'

The critical architectural claim is not merely that these are three states, but that they are *nested*: access to ventral vagal functioning requires *suppression* of the more primitive circuits, and loss of ventral vagal tone triggers automatic cascade into sympathetic mobilization and, if that fails, dorsal shutdown. This is the topology of nested attractors — each basin is embedded within a larger one, and the escape routes between them are asymmetric.

The evidence supporting this nesting logic includes:

- **WS3-SP** (Tier 1, established confidence): 'For true trust in relationships to be built, individuals need to be in the safety bucket (ventral vagal state).' This is not a facilitating condition — it is a *prerequisite*. Trust cannot be cognitively willed into existence from a sympathetically activated baseline. This is functionally identical to saying the relational safety basin must be entered before certain cognitive/relational operations become possible — the signature of a hierarchical attractor structure.

- **WS2-SP** (Tier 2, high confidence): The 'inside-out' regulatory model posits that internal neural regulatory state mediates the impact of external triggers, rather than external triggers directly causing outcomes. This is structurally identical to the predictive processing claim that the brain's generative model (internal prior) shapes how sensory evidence is interpreted. The relational safety state, in this framing, is not a *response* to a safe environment — it is the *lens through which* the environment is evaluated as safe or threatening.

- **WS4-SP** (Tier 2, established confidence): The Safe and Sound Protocol achieves autonomic state shifts through *acoustic frequency filtering* — specifically prosodic frequency bands associated with mammalian vocalizations. The fact that the nervous system can be 'unlocked' into ventral vagal states through sub-symbolic, pre-linguistic, acoustic signals strongly implies that the relational safety basin was laid down before language, before cognition, before explicit social knowledge. Its depth is not merely metaphorical — it is a developmental and evolutionary depth.

### 2. McGilchrist's Hemispheric Hierarchy: Holism as Primary, Fragmentation as Secondary

Iain McGilchrist's work on hemispheric specialization provides a complementary, neuroanatomical angle on the same structural question. The right hemisphere, which specializes in broad contextual attention, holistic pattern recognition, relational attunement, embodied presence, and implicit knowing, is argued to be both phylogenetically and functionally primary. The left hemisphere's narrow, focal, sequential, categorical processing style is secondary — derivative of and dependent upon the right hemisphere's broader frame.

- **WS3-IM** (Tier 2, high confidence): Right hemisphere damage produces hemispatial neglect — not merely the loss of a processing style, but the collapse of the organism's capacity to hold a coherent world-field. The remaining left-hemisphere processing is not adaptive — it is literally half-blind, unable to shift attention within the narrow field it can still access. This is structurally analogous to a polyvagal dorsal state: the organism is 'alive' but has lost the broad attentional bandwidth that makes flexible response possible.

- **WS2-IM** (Tier 2, high confidence): The right hemisphere holds the broader, more contextually embedded mode of attention — the one that tracks relationships, faces, prosody, and whole-field patterns. If the right hemisphere's processing style is mapped onto the polyvagal hierarchy, it corresponds to ventral vagal / social engagement: the state that holds the *context* within which specific stimuli are interpreted. The left hemisphere's narrow, sequential processing style maps onto sympathetic mobilization: useful for targeted action, but catastrophic as a default state.

This hemispheric mapping is speculative but structurally coherent: both polyvagal theory and McGilchrist's hemispheric model propose a hierarchy in which the relational/contextual/holistic mode is the *organizing frame* and the focused/mobilization mode is *embedded within* it.

### 3. Dynamic Stability Through Recursive Feedback

- **WS2-IM** (dynamic stability through recursive loops and spirals): Biological systems maintain identity through continuous dynamic interactions characterized by recursive loops and spirals, not through static structures or linear causation. This is the mathematical vocabulary of attractor theory: the identity of a system is defined not by its position but by the *geometry of its return dynamics* — the basin that it repeatedly finds its way back to after perturbation.

This provides the formal bridge between the phenomenological observation ('this organism keeps returning to a dysregulated state') and the dynamical systems claim ('this organism's attractor landscape has been deformed such that a dysregulated state is now the lowest basin'). Trauma, in this framing, is not a memory or a habit — it is an alteration of attractor geometry.

## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A (Tier 1 — Empirical Science)
**Polyvagal hierarchy as functional attractor nesting**

Ventral vagal safety is empirically established as a *prerequisite* state for trust, social engagement, therapeutic change, and play — not merely a desirable state. This prerequisite relationship is the functional signature of a hierarchical attractor: you cannot reach certain states without first entering the basin that contains them. The polyvagal hierarchy is therefore not merely a phylogenetic description but a dynamical one: the three states have asymmetric transition probabilities (easier to fall from safety into mobilization than to return; easier to fall from mobilization into shutdown than to return), characteristic of nested basins with asymmetric energy barriers.

**Falsification condition**: If individuals can construct robust relational trust while in chronic sympathetic activation (demonstrably without ventral vagal access), the prerequisite claim fails.

### Hypothesis B (Tier 2 — Cross-Framework Synthesis)
**Relational safety as master prior in a hierarchical generative model**

Integrating polyvagal theory with predictive processing (Friston) and allostasis (McEwen), the nervous system is a hierarchical generative model where the deepest prior — the prior that all others condition upon — is the answer to the question: 'Is there a co-regulating other present, or am I alone with threat?' This prior is deepest because it was the earliest evolutionary solution to the metabolic uncertainty problem for social mammals: co-regulation with conspecifics is more energy-efficient than solo allostatic management. All downstream setpoints (HPA axis tone, HRV, immune calibration, pain threshold, sleep architecture) are nested within this relational prior and inherit its error signal when it is chronically unresolved.

**Falsification condition**: If allostatic biomarkers are equally well predicted by non-relational variables independently of social safety measures, the 'master prior' claim weakens to 'important prior among several.'

### Hypothesis C (Tier 3 — Speculative)
**Relational safety as topological invariant of self-organizing biological systems**

Any system capable of modeling itself (autopoiesis + self-prediction) requires an attractor state in which the self-other boundary is permeable-but-coherent. This state — ventral vagal in Porges's terms, right-hemisphere holism in McGilchrist's terms, witnessing presence in contemplative terms — is the physical instantiation of the free-energy minimum for a social organism. Trauma is a *topological deformation* of this basin: the self-other boundary becomes either collapsed (dorsal freeze/dissociation) or hyperrigid (sympathetic hypervigilance). Healing is topological restoration of basin geometry, not content processing.

**Falsification condition**: Effective trauma resolution achieved purely through content processing without relational container, OR multiscale entropy analysis of HRV showing no special geometric distinction between ventral vagal and other states.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A
Polyvagal theory's neuroanatomical foundations have been challenged: Grossman & Taylor (2007) argued that the myelinated/unmyelinated vagal distinction does not map cleanly onto evolutionary stages, and that the dorsal vagal 'immobilization' system in humans is more complex than Porges's early formulations suggested. If the phylogenetic ladder is architecturally flawed, the attractor hierarchy loses its evolutionary grounding. However, even critics generally accept the *functional* three-state model — the phenomenological hierarchy holds even if the substrate is more complex than originally proposed.

### Against Hypothesis B
The free energy / predictive processing framework has not yet produced consensus on *which level* of the hierarchical generative model is 'deepest.' Strong candidates include: interoceptive predictions (older than social prediction phylogenetically), homeostatic predictions (temperature, glucose), and social predictions (more recently evolved but more energetically efficient for social mammals). The allostatic literature suggests that chronic social threat (isolation, low SES) produces the broadest biomarker disruption — which supports social prediction as the master prior — but this remains correlation, not mechanism.

### Against Hypothesis C
'Topological deformation of a basin' is a powerful metaphor that risks being unfalsifiable unless translated into precise measurable quantities. The spirit-density and soul-density mirror evidence, while structurally evocative, is interpretive synthesis rather than empirical data. The hypothesis needs a measurement bridge: multiscale entropy of autonomic time series, phase-space reconstruction of HRV, or dynamical complexity indices could potentially operationalize 'basin geometry' in testable ways.

## Synthesis

The evidence available — primarily from Porges and McGilchrist — converges moderately on the hierarchical attractor model. The strongest convergence is structural rather than directly empirical: both frameworks independently identify a state (ventral vagal / right-hemisphere holism) that is:

1. **Prerequisite rather than optional** for certain higher-order functions
2. **Accessible through archaic, pre-cognitive channels** (acoustic prosody, facial expression, touch), implying evolutionary depth
3. **Subject to hysteresis-like dynamics** (easier to lose than to regain, consistent with asymmetric basin structure)
4. **Organized through recursive feedback** rather than linear control, consistent with attractor geometry

The integration with Friston's free-energy principle remains the most intellectually productive but empirically underdeveloped arm of the triangulation. The key insight it contributes — even from structural inference — is the *prior on priors* logic: if the nervous system is a hierarchical generative model, then the deepest priors are the ones that all other predictions are conditioned upon, and the deepest possible prior for a social organism is the answer to 'is relational safety available?' because it determines the metabolic budget available for all downstream regulatory processes.

The allostatic load literature (McEwen) provides epidemiological support without mechanistic specification: the consistent finding that chronic social threat (isolation, discrimination, relational instability) produces the widest and deepest disruption of allostatic biomarkers — wider than non-relational stressors of comparable intensity — is the population-level signature of a master setpoint being chronically displaced.

## Implications

### Clinical
If relational safety is the deepest attractor basin, trauma treatment that targets content (memories, beliefs, meanings) without first restoring the basin architecture is working in the wrong order. The therapeutic relationship itself is not merely supportive context — it *is* the primary intervention, because it is the vehicle for entering the ventral vagal basin within which all other processing becomes possible. This reframes the therapeutic alliance from a 'non-specific factor' to the primary mechanism of change.

### Psychopharmacology
Psychiatric medications that target downstream setpoints (cortisol, serotonin, norepinephrine) without addressing the master prior will produce partial and fragile results, consistent with clinical observation. Medications that shift autonomic state directly (MDMA's temporary ventral vagal induction; ketamine's dissociation of trauma content from threat-state; beta-blockers' sympathetic dampening) may work partly by temporarily altering basin accessibility.

### Pedagogy and Organizational Design
Any learning, creative, or collaborative environment that operates primarily in sympathetic activation (deadline pressure, threat of failure, competitive comparison) is, by this model, working against the attractor geometry required for the cognitive operations it is trying to produce. Psychological safety in organizations and classrooms is not a 'soft' consideration — it is the basin requirement for higher-order cognition.

### Soul / Spirit Dimensions
The soul-density mirror entry captures something important: the therapeutic recognition that a client 'lacks the substrate for defense' rather than 'failing to defend' shifts the clinical frame entirely. Rebuilding 'relational NAD+' — the internal currency of self-regard, boundary integrity, and nervous system coherence — is the soul-level analog of restoring attractor basin depth. The spirit-density mirror goes further: if the deepest attractor is a state of 'witnessing presence' — a non-conceptual awareness that can hold both self and other without collapsing into either — then spiritual practice traditions that cultivate this state may be operating on the deepest basin directly, bypassing the pharmacological, relational, and somatic access routes.

## Open Questions

1. **Interoception vs. social safety**: Which is the deeper prior — homeostatic/interoceptive predictions or social safety predictions? The phylogenetic argument favors interoception; the allostatic load epidemiology favors social safety. Is there an empirical adjudication?

2. **Measurement of basin geometry**: Can multiscale entropy, fractal dimension, or phase-space reconstruction of HRV/EEG time series serve as operational proxies for 'basin depth'? If so, does ventral vagal state show measurably deeper/wider basin geometry than sympathetic or dorsal states?

3. **The over-weighted social prior problem**: Some trauma survivors seem to have a *hyperactive* social threat prior — they are exquisitely sensitive to relational cues and locked *out* of the safety basin in objectively safe relationships. This is the opposite of the simple 'depletion' model. How does the hierarchical attractor framework handle states of *excessive* prior precision in the threat direction?

4. **McGilchrist-Porges correspondence**: Does the right hemisphere constitute the neural substrate of the social engagement system? Is left-hemisphere dominance (common in Western educated populations) a structural factor in the epidemic of autonomic dysregulation?
