Mechanistic adjacency is not mechanistic identity. The tryptophan → melatonin → slow-wave → glymphatic chain exists in the literature, but Pearl having the tryptophan node doesn't mean a user asking about sleep clearance will receive useful information. Without the integrating concept — AQP4-mediated bulk flow, driven by arterial pulsation and slow oscillatory pressure gradients — the chain cannot close. *Defense:* The solution is not to deny the fragments but to explicitly flag them as incomplete chains requiring integration. Adding Nedergaard literature would complete, not contradict, Pearl's existing content.

### Against Hypothesis B
The three-density / oscillatory correspondence may be a compelling post-hoc analogy without predictive power. Slow oscillations are not 'body' in any reductionist sense — they involve thalamocortical loops, basal ganglia, brainstem nuclei, and astrocytic networks simultaneously. Mapping phenomenological categories onto frequency bands risks creating a false sense of explanatory closure.

*Defense:* The hypothesis does not require reductionist identity — only structural isomorphism at the level of function. If body-density practices (sleep hygiene, nutrition, movement) reliably increase slow-wave power, and soul-density practices (relational repair, therapeutic processing) reliably increase sleep spindle density, and spirit-density practices (meditation, contemplation) reliably modulate gamma coherence, then the correspondence has predictive content even without mechanistic identity.

### Against Hypothesis C
The claim that Pearl 'systematically underestimates rest states across all domains' is difficult to falsify without a comprehensive audit. It risks becoming an unfalsifiable meta-hypothesis that attributes all Pearl limitations to this single gap.

*Defense:* The claim can be made specific: Pearl's Restoration operation lacks a mechanistic account of how rest produces restoration.
