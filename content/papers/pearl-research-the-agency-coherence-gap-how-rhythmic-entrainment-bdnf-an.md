# The Agency-Coherence Gap: Rhythmic Entrainment, BDNF, and Meaning-Making in Neurological Rehabilitation

## Abstract

This document synthesizes available evidence to generate competing hypotheses about the mechanisms and requirements of effective neurological rehabilitation, with particular attention to traumatic brain injury (TBI) and the role of music-based interventions. Analysis across body, soul, and spirit densities reveals a consistent cross-scale pattern: volitional agency (the capacity to act) degrades faster than structural substrate and self-concept. This 'agency-coherence gap' has direct mechanistic and therapeutic implications. Three hypotheses are developed: (A) Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation works via external temporal scaffolding compensated by BDNF-mediated neuroplasticity; (B) therapeutic alliance is a necessary biological co-factor, not a contextual variable; (C) music functions as a controlled identity-state perturbation tool, preventing premature attractor settlement. Critical knowledge gaps are identified, including absent Tier 1 entries on auditory-premotor cortex connectivity and qualitative meaning-making research.

## Evidence Review

### The Agency-Coherence Gap: A Cross-Density Pattern

The most striking finding across the available evidence is a consistent pattern appearing at three distinct levels of analysis. At the body level, WS3-RP establishes that strength (functional output) declines faster than muscle mass (structural substrate) with age. This is not merely a physiological curiosity — it reveals that the functional-structural relationship is non-linear, with functional capacity being the more fragile variable.

The fractal mirrors then reveal that this same pattern repeats at higher scales. At the soul density, the mirror describes the gap between ego-ideal and executive function: the person who retains a coherent self-image as capable while losing the actual capacity to regulate impulse into directed action. At the spirit density, the witness (consciousness that knows) persists while the will (consciousness that acts) recedes. Identity and agency are not coextensive.

For neurological rehabilitation — particularly TBI — this pattern is not merely analogical but mechanistically predictive. TBI patients characteristically retain (often painfully intact) awareness of who they were before injury, while losing the motor initiation, cognitive flexibility, and executive coordination that once animated that identity. The gap between knowing and doing is the clinical wound. Any rehabilitation framework that addresses only the structural (motor practice) or only the cognitive (self-awareness) misses the essential dynamic.

### CNS Integration as the Target Mechanism

WS2-PA-Transduction establishes that the central nervous system coordinates neuromuscular activity by integrating multiple sensory channels: proprioception, balance, visual, and vestibular input. This multi-channel integration generates precise motor commands and maintains postural stability. The entry is rated Tier 2/established confidence.

This is the direct mechanistic substrate that Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation (RAS) would engage. RAS adds a rhythmic auditory channel to a sensorimotor integration system whose internal temporal reference has been degraded by injury. From a control theory perspective, this is equivalent to providing an external frequency reference to an oscillator that has lost phase coherence — the external signal can re-entrain the system without requiring the damaged internal oscillator to fully repair itself first.

The signal processing lens refines this: RAS does not require the auditory cortex to 'teach' the motor cortex. The anticipated finding from auditory-premotor fMRI research (not yet present in Pearl's knowledge base as Tier 1) would be that auditory-premotor connectivity provides a direct neural bridge, allowing rhythmic anticipation generated in auditory processing areas to pre-activate motor planning circuits. In TBI, this bridge may be partially preserved even when internal motor timing is disrupted — which would explain RAS efficacy.

### BDNF as Molecular Bridge

WS2-RP-Synthesis establishes that BDNF can be released from muscle cells (Myo-BDNF) and locally or systemically promotes muscle repair and neural growth. This entry is rated Tier 2/high confidence.

The hypothesis that music-accompanied movement would produce BDNF upregulation rests on two connections: (1) rhythmic movement during music therapy would trigger Myo-BDNF release, and (2) this BDNF would contribute to the neural consolidation of patterns being practiced during the session. The first connection is well-supported. The second requires an assumption — that peripherally released BDNF reaches central targets in sufficient quantity — that is plausible but requires Tier 1 verification specifically in TBI populations.

The more conservative version of this hypothesis holds that music therapy works by motivating sustained engagement in physical movement practice, and it is the movement (with its established central BDNF effects from exercise physiology) rather than the music per se that drives neuroplasticity. Even this conservative version has therapeutic implications: music therapy's role may be to increase the dose of effective movement practice by reducing the subjective effort and increasing engagement, rather than having a unique neural mechanism.

### Relational Holding as Biological Necessity

Two entries converge on the importance of relational context in therapeutic effectiveness. WS4-SP-Regulation describes polyvagal-informed therapy's goal of re-embodiment through cultivation of self-awareness of physiological states, achieved within a safe relational context. WS4-BK-Conduction describes trauma-focused psychotherapy's emphasis on verbalization, relational support, and finding words for internal experience.

The EPA/DHA fractal mirror (soul density) captures the core insight: no single relational nutrient is sufficient. The person who cannot efficiently convert received care into internal coherence requires both direct emotional contact and structural holding. This maps onto what therapeutic alliance research in physical rehabilitation increasingly suggests: the relationship between patient and therapist predicts outcome independently of technique fidelity, not because alliance replaces technique but because without it, technique is never applied consistently enough to produce lasting change.

For TBI specifically, this has added valence: TBI often involves profound disruption to the patient's sense of relational continuity. The injury interrupts the social narrative of selfhood. Rehabilitation that provides only motor practice without relational meaning-making may succeed technically while failing existentially — and the existential failure may limit the technical success through reduced compliance, motivation, and neuroplastic receptivity.

### The Psychedelic Analogy and Identity-State Perturbation

WS4-SH-Regulation describes psychedelic-assisted therapy as enabling examination of core beliefs that constrain action — beliefs that have become rigid and self-reinforcing. The mechanism proposed is that the psychedelic state temporarily dissolves the normal self-model, creating a window during which new patterns of belief and behavior can be installed with reduced resistance.

This is speculative as applied to music, but the chaos attractor lens makes it analytically coherent. Post-TBI rehabilitation frequently stalls at what appears to be a plateau — but plateau may be a misnomer. What clinicians observe may be an attractor state: the damaged nervous system has found a stable compensatory configuration that resists perturbation. Motor practice within this attractor state produces diminishing returns because the system keeps returning to the same equilibrium.

Music's capacity to produce rapid, reliable state shifts — across emotional, attentional, and autonomic dimensions — makes it a candidate entropy-injection tool. By temporarily increasing system disorder (in the precise thermodynamic sense: reducing the probability that the system remains in its current state), music may create a window of enhanced neuroplastic receptivity during which motor practice can establish patterns in a less pre-committed state space.

The spirit-density mirror on the strength/agency gap is relevant here: consciousness retains its self-concept while volitional current thins. Post-TBI patients frequently construct a rigid self-model organized around their deficits — 'I am someone who cannot walk normally,' 'I am someone who loses words.' This self-model is not merely psychological; it may have neural correlates in default mode network activity that actively interferes with motor learning by generating predictions of failure. Music may temporarily suppress this predictive self-modeling, analogously (not identically) to what 5-HT2A agonists do in psychedelic therapy.

## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: The External Oscillator Model (Tier 1)

Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation compensates for degraded internal CNS timing by providing an external temporal scaffold that re-entrains motor planning circuits, with BDNF upregulation via concurrent rhythmic movement serving as the molecular mechanism of durable neuroplastic change.

This hypothesis is conservative and mechanistically grounded. It does not require music to do anything 'special' beyond providing a reliable temporal signal. The control theory framework predicts that a system with a degraded internal oscillator will entrain to an external reference if the reference is sufficiently stable and within the system's natural frequency range. RAS protocols appear to exploit exactly this.

What would falsify it: If RAS shows no advantage over non-rhythmic auditory stimulation of equal complexity (e.g., speech or noise at matched intensity), the temporal specificity hypothesis fails. If BDNF does not rise with music-accompanied movement versus movement alone in controlled trials, the molecular mechanism is not present.

### Hypothesis B: The Alliance Co-Factor Model (Tier 2)

Therapeutic alliance functions as a necessary neurobiological co-factor in music therapy and physical rehabilitation — not a contextual variable or motivational lubricant, but a direct modulator of neuroplastic receptivity through polyvagal state regulation.

This hypothesis elevates alliance from a process variable to a mechanism. The polyvagal framework predicts that ventral vagal state (social engagement, safety) enables the parasympathetic flexibility required for neuroplasticity, while chronic sympathetic activation or dorsal vagal shutdown (states common in TBI) suppresses it. A therapist who reliably induces and maintains ventral vagal state in the patient would be performing a direct neurobiological function.

What would falsify it: If therapeutic alliance scores show no predictive relationship with rehabilitation outcome when controlling for session frequency, technique fidelity, and patient characteristics, the specific alliance mechanism fails. If polyvagal state during sessions (measured via HRV) does not correlate with weekly functional gains, the proposed mechanism is not operative.

### Hypothesis C: The Entropy-Injection Model (Tier 3)

Music in neurological rehabilitation functions as a controlled identity-state perturbation — temporarily reducing the rigidity of the post-TBI self-model and increasing neuroplastic receptivity by injecting controlled entropy into a nervous system trapped in a compensatory attractor state.

This is the most speculative hypothesis but the most novel. It predicts a specific temporal structure to music therapy efficacy: the gains should be disproportionately concentrated in the immediate post-music period rather than accumulating linearly across the session. It also predicts that the emotional and identity-relevant properties of the music would matter for outcome, not just its rhythmic properties — which distinguishes it from Hypothesis A.

What would falsify it: If within-session motor practice shows no temporal clustering of successful trials immediately following high-arousal music exposure, the entropy-injection mechanism is not present. If musically naive or emotionally unresponsive patients show equivalent gains to highly music-responsive patients, the identity-state modulation mechanism is not operative.

## Debate and Objections

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection is that the Myo-BDNF mechanism (WS2-RP) was established for muscle repair, not neural repair. Extrapolating to TBI neuroplasticity requires that (a) exercise during music therapy is of sufficient intensity and duration to produce BDNF upregulation, and (b) that upregulation reaches central targets. Rehabilitation sessions may not meet the exercise thresholds established in healthy populations, and TBI itself may alter the blood-brain barrier in ways that affect BDNF trafficking.

The strongest support is the mechanistic coherence with the CNS integration entry. This is not analogy — it is the same neural system.

### Against Hypothesis B

Alliance is correlated with positive outcomes across virtually every therapy modality studied — which may mean it reflects common factors in therapeutic competence (attentive therapists execute technique better AND form better alliances) rather than a separable mechanism. The polyvagal theoretical framework, while influential, remains contested in neuroscience; polyvagal theory's specific claims about distinct vagal circuits have been challenged.

The strongest support is practical: in long-term rehabilitation, dropout and non-adherence are the primary causes of failure, and alliance is the strongest predictor of continued engagement. Even as a motivational mechanism rather than a neurobiological one, it may be rate-limiting.

### Against Hypothesis C

The psychedelic analogy is at risk of being metaphorical rather than mechanistic. Music and psychedelics produce their state effects through entirely different molecular pathways. Default mode network suppression by psilocybin (the proposed psychedelic mechanism for loosening self-model rigidity) is not replicated by music in the same pattern. The hypothesis may be pattern-matching across domains without valid mechanistic transfer.

The strongest support is the chaos attractor framework: plateau states in rehabilitation are real, empirically observed phenomena that do not respond well to 'more of the same.' Any intervention that reliably produces rapid, broad-spectrum state shifts is a candidate attractor-escape tool, regardless of mechanism.

## Synthesis

The three hypotheses are not mutually exclusive; they operate at different levels of analysis and different timescales. A complete model of music therapy in TBI rehabilitation might require all three:

1. **Session-level mechanism (Hypothesis A):** RAS provides the moment-to-moment temporal scaffold that enables motor practice to occur with correct timing. BDNF consolidates the practiced patterns.

2. **Trajectory-level mechanism (Hypothesis B):** Alliance maintains the conditions (safety, engagement, meaning) under which sessions continue long enough for BDNF and motor consolidation to accumulate.

3. **Plateau-escape mechanism (Hypothesis C):** Periodic identity-state perturbation via emotionally engaging music prevents the rehabilitating nervous system from settling into deficit-organized attractor states.

The integrated model predicts that the most effective music therapy protocols would:
- Use rhythmically precise, externally paced music for the movement practice phase (A)
- Incorporate explicit meaning-making conversations before and after sessions (B)
- Deliberately select music with high personal and emotional significance, not just rhythmic regularity, at specific points in the session arc (C)
