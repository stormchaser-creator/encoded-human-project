## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Conservative (Tier 1 — Published Science)
**Claim:** Endogenous DC electric fields at wound margins function as primary morphogenetic organizers of epithelial and connective tissue migration, operating upstream of and in parallel with biochemical signaling cascades, with the HPA stress response modulating wound field magnitude through catecholamine-driven changes in transepithelial potential.

This is the most defensible hypothesis because it rests on established experimental biology (Zhao 2006, McCaig reviews) and connects to the HPA-immune cascade through a mechanistically plausible pathway: cortisol and catecholamines are known to alter ion channel expression (ENaC, Na+/K+ ATPase activity) in epithelial tissue, which would directly modulate transepithelial potential and therefore wound field magnitude. The HPA-immune cascade entry (decoded-4f2233cbcd0d) documents cortisol's immunosuppressive effects on wound healing — the Hypothesis A extension is that this operates through dual mechanisms: (a) cytokine suppression (established) and (b) bioelectric field dampening (plausible, requiring direct experimental confirmation).

The control theory lens is particularly useful here: the wound field functions as a setpoint-maintaining feedback system. Injury creates a step-change in transepithelial potential (the wound short-circuits the epithelial battery). This voltage gradient is the error signal that drives corrective cellular migration. HPA dysregulation that chronically elevates glucocorticoids would alter the gain of this feedback system, potentially explaining why chronic stress impairs wound healing beyond what cytokine suppression alone accounts for.

### Hypothesis B: Integrative (Tier 2 — Cross-Tradition Synthesis)
**Claim:** The body's electromagnetic field architecture — spanning DC wound fields, cardiac EM fields (HRV coherence), and neural bioelectric gradients — constitutes an integrated morphogenetic communication network that the autonomic nervous system regulates in real time, such that practices targeting vagal tone (breathwork, somatic pendulation) simultaneously reorganize bioelectric topology at tissue level.

This hypothesis requires a more complex signal pathway: psychological state → ANS outflow → peripheral nerve terminal activity → neuropeptide release at tissue-nerve junctions → ion channel modulation → transepithelial potential change → wound field reorganization. Each link in this chain has experimental support, but the complete chain has not been demonstrated in a single experimental system.

The critical intermediate link — peripheral neuropeptide release modulating tissue bioelectric fields — is actually the most defensible component. Substance P, VIP (vasoactive intestinal peptide), and neuropeptide Y are released from autonomic and sensory nerve terminals at wound margins and are known to modulate epithelial ion channel conductance. This creates a biochemically concrete pathway from ANS state to wound field magnitude that does not require any speculative electromagnetic transmission.

The entropy lens is useful here: chronic sympathetic dominance (high entropy in the autonomic sense — loss of HRV complexity) would drive persistent bioelectric noise in wound fields, disrupting the coherent gradient that directs epithelial migration. Restoration of HRV complexity through breathwork would correspond to restoration of wound field coherence — a decrease in local entropy that enables organized morphogenetic work.

### Hypothesis C: Radical (Tier 3 — Speculative)
**Claim:** Consciousness-level interventions modulate tissue-level bioelectric fields through a top-down information cascade, such that the 'Energetic Body' (Body 9) describes a literally measurable electromagnetic topology encoding morphogenetic identity, trauma memory, and healing trajectory simultaneously.

This is the most speculative hypothesis and requires the most careful handling. The key question is whether the claim is (a) that consciousness modulates bioelectric fields through established physiological pathways (ANS → neuropeptide → ion channel), or (b) that consciousness itself carries electromagnetic properties that directly couple to tissue fields. Version (a) is Tier 2 and is subsumed by Hypothesis B. Version (b) is Tier 3 and requires a biophysical framework for which there is currently insufficient evidence.

The strongest empirical anchor for the radical version is Becker's documentation that acupuncture needle insertion at classic meridian points — which correspond to anatomical pathways of low skin resistance — produces DC current changes at distance. If physical insertion at these topological singularities modulates distant field states, the question of whether directed somatic attention (which alters efferent ANS outflow to the same peripheral regions) can do the same becomes empirically tractable.

Levin's (2021) framework is relevant but requires careful interpretation: his claim that bioelectric networks implement 'cognitive' processing is about information processing in tissue, not about consciousness as a field emitter. Collapsing this distinction would be a category error. However, if we accept that directed somatic attention modulates ANS outflow to specific peripheral regions (empirically established), and that this ANS outflow modulates local bioelectric fields (Hypothesis B), then the chain from directed awareness to tissue field topology is complete — without requiring any non-physical mechanism.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A
The strongest objection is that bioelectric fields and biochemical gradients may be inseparable — both are expressions of the same underlying ion channel activity, and attempting to separate 'upstream' and 'downstream' may impose a false sequencing on simultaneous processes. The field is not prior to the chemistry; they are co-instantiated. This objection has merit but doesn't undermine the clinical implication: regardless of causal sequencing, interventions that restore wound field coherence (applied EFs, bioelectric dressings) produce healing outcomes that exceed what pharmacological cytokine modulation alone achieves.

### Against Hypothesis B
The quantitative plausibility of direct cardiac EM field → tissue coupling is weak. The heart generates fields of ~10 μT at the body surface, decaying rapidly with distance and tissue attenuation. The minimum field strength demonstrated to direct cell migration (Zhao 2006) is ~10 mV/mm, which corresponds to a much larger field than cardiac emission would produce at distal tissue. However, as noted, the indirect pathway (ANS → neuropeptide → ion channel → field) is quantitatively plausible and preserves the functional claim without requiring implausible field transmission.

### Against Hypothesis C
The radical version risks conflating metaphorical and literal uses of 'electromagnetic.' When somatic traditions describe the 'energetic body,' they may not be making a literal claim about measurable fields but rather using field metaphors to describe patterns of experience, affect, and sensation that have distributed topological properties. Prematurely literalizing these descriptions — without direct experimental evidence linking conscious attention to tissue bioelectric changes — would introduce a category error into the knowledge base. The appropriate epistemic stance is to treat the correspondence as a generative hypothesis requiring direct experimental testing rather than an established fact.
