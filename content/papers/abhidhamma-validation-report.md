## 5. Tier A Scoring Validation — Unit-by-Unit

### A-ADH-001 (Five jhāna factors as dissociable)

- **C1 (consciousness-specific reference corpus):** VALID. Attentional orienting, maintenance, and reward valence are squarely in consciousness/attention neuroscience. Not general biology.
- **C2 (independent derivation):** VALID. ~2300 years separation. Pre-modern text. Automatically satisfied.
- **C3 (predictive value beyond existing neuroscience):** **SCRUTINIZE.** The claim is that FIVE factors are simultaneously present and dissociable. Attention research has identified ~3 dissociable attentional networks (Posner's alerting, orienting, executive). The Abhidhamma claims 5. Does the additional granularity (adding pīti/sukha as separate from attentional components) genuinely predict something beyond Posner? **Verdict: MARGINAL PASS.** The addition of hedonic and energetic components as dissociable FROM attentional components in absorption states is not trivially predicted by attention research. But Pearl should strengthen the argument for why 5 > 3 is a genuine prediction, not just finer slicing.
- **C4 (falsification paths):** VALID. Multi-variate EEG/fMRI dissociation study is feasible. Falsifiable from both directions.
- **Overall: TIER A — CONFIRMED** but C3 argument needs tightening in v7.

### A-ADH-003 (Invariant sequential factor elimination)

- **C1:** VALID.
- **C2:** VALID.
- **C3:** **STRONG PASS.** This is the strongest C3 in the corpus. An invariant ORDER of neural signature desynchronization is a precise, non-trivial prediction. Automatization models predict reduced effort over time, but do NOT predict a specific elimination sequence matching vitarka → vichara → pīti → sukha. This genuinely exceeds existing neuroscience predictions.
- **C4:** VALID. The sequence is directly falsifiable — wrong order = Abhidhamma taxonomy wrong.
- **Overall: TIER A — CONFIRMED.** Pearl's assessment that this is the strongest unit is correct.

### A-ADH-006 (Sati vs. paññā as distinct)

- **C1:** VALID. Meta-awareness vs. insight is a recognized distinction in contemplative neuroscience.
- **C2:** VALID.
- **C3:** **SCRUTINIZE.** Pearl cites Dunne, Lutz, and Davidson as having "argued for this distinction." If they've already argued for it, does C3 still hold? **Verdict: MARGINAL PASS.** The key is directionality — the Abhidhamma formalized it >1800 years earlier, and the prediction that training one doesn't transfer to the other has NOT been directly tested. The transfer prediction saves C3.
- **C4:** VALID. Training study with transfer tests is feasible.
- **Overall: TIER A — CONFIRMED** but Pearl should note in v7 that the retrospective correspondence with Dunne/Lutz/Davidson means C3 is weaker here than in prospective units.

### A-ADH-009 (Nimitta phase transition)

- **C1:** VALID. Phase transitions in neural dynamics during meditation induction.

- **C4:** VALID. Neurofeedback during jhāna induction with nimitta reporting is feasible if technically challenging.
