## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: AQP4-Mediated Glymphatic Heterogeneity as IL-6 Clearance Confounder (Tier 1 — Mechanistic Inference)

**Claim**: AQP4 polymorphisms produce clinically significant inter-individual differences in CSF IL-6 clearance rate following aneurysmal SAH. This means that a fixed IL-6 threshold for antifibrinolytic cessation will systematically overtreate slow-clearers (high IL-6 due to poor glymphatic efflux, not high inflammatory burden) and undertreate fast-clearers (normalized IL-6 despite ongoing cortical spreading depression).

**Mechanism**: AQP4 water channels at astrocytic endfeet create the osmotic gradient driving glymphatic flux. Loss-of-function AQP4 variants reduce this gradient, slowing the convective flow that carries IL-6 from brain interstitium into the CSF and ultimately into cervical lymphatics. In SAH, where subarachnoid blood directly disrupts glymphatic dynamics at the glia limitans, AQP4 function determines the residual clearance capacity during the critical 72-96 hour window when antifibrinolytic decisions are made.

**Prediction**: AQP4 rs3763040 and rs9951307 (loss-of-function variants associated with amyloid accumulation) will associate with prolonged CSF IL-6 elevation trajectories independent of clinical bleed grade, and will predict DCI through the mechanism of inflammatory mediator accumulation rather than delayed vasospasm per se.

**Analytical Lenses Applied**:
- *Network theory*: AQP4 is the rate-limiting hub in the glymphatic clearance network; variants create quantifiable bottlenecks
- *Control theory*: AQP4 function sets the gain of the IL-6 feedback loop — low gain (poor clearance) produces falsely elevated setpoints
- *Information theory*: Low-AQP4-function patients generate high IL-6 readings that carry low information about ongoing inflammatory activity — the signal is amplified by the clearance defect, not by inflammatory intensity

**Falsifiable by**: A prospective SAH cohort (n>100) showing no significant difference in CSF IL-6 half-life between AQP4 functional vs. loss-of-function genotype groups after controlling for bleed volume, Fisher grade, and VCI status.

### Hypothesis C: VCI as External Glymphatic Surrogate — A Nonlinear Three-Variable Control System (Tier 3 — Speculative)

**Claim**: VCI (ventricular catheter irrigation) functions as a mechanical surrogate for glymphatic clearance during the acute post-SAH period when endogenous glymphatic function is maximally disrupted by subarachnoid blood, intracranial hypertension, and neuroinflammation. **Mechanism**:

- AQP4 genotype determines the speed of endogenous recovery, defining the length of this potential clearance gap
- During the clearance gap, inflammatory mediators accumulate and DCI risk is highest — antifibrinolytic continuation during this specific window may be most protective

**Analytical Lenses Applied**:
- *Coupled oscillators*: VCI washout rate and glymphatic flux recovery are two desynchronized clearance oscillators; their phase relationship determines the true inflammatory exposure integral
- *Signal processing*: VCI acts as a low-pass filter on CSF IL-6 — it suppresses high-frequency inflammatory spikes, potentially masking the DCI-predictive signal

**Falsifiable by**: A within-patient study showing that CSF IL-6 measured immediately before vs. 30/60/120 minutes into VCI pause (to allow equilibration) is statistically equivalent — which would indicate VCI does not significantly confound IL-6 measurements.

## Debate and Objection Analysis

### Hypothesis A Debate

**Strongest objection**: AQP4 clearance-rate differences that matter for chronic amyloid accumulation (measured over years) may be physiologically negligible over the acute 72-96 hour antifibrinolytic window. A 10-20% difference in glymphatic flux could shift IL-6 clearance kinetics by minutes, not hours — insufficient to matter for a threshold-based protocol with reasonable confidence intervals around the threshold.

**Counter**: AQP4 knockout mouse experiments show 60-70% reduction in glymphatic tracer clearance — not 10-20%. If human loss-of-function variants produce even 30-40% reduction in acute clearance capacity, this would shift IL-6 half-life from approximately 4 hours to 6+ hours, meaning a patient sampled at 12 hours post-ictus would show IL-6 levels equivalent to a wild-type patient sampled at 8 hours. This is a clinically significant confound.

### Hypothesis B Debate

**Strongest objection**: The Planck principle is descriptively accurate but normatively useless — it cannot distinguish between protocols that persist because of institutional inertia versus protocols that persist because they are genuinely adequate. The 72-hour antifibrinolytic protocol may be evidence-adequate even if it was never formally optimized. The burden of proof lies with demonstrating that biomarker-guided approaches are superior, not with explaining why fixed-duration approaches persist.

**Counter**: The VO2 max non-responder data provides an evidence-grounded prior, not merely a metaphor. If 40% of the population systematically fails to respond to fixed-duration exercise guidelines, and if the biological individuality principle generalizes across physiological systems (a standard assumption in precision medicine), then the null hypothesis should be that fixed-duration antifibrinolytic protocols are inadequate for a similar fraction of SAH patients — not that they are adequate until proven otherwise.

## Synthesis: The Integrated Control-Theory Framework

Across all three hypotheses, a coherent architectural picture emerges:

**The SAH neuroinflammatory system as a control problem**:
- *Plant*: CSF inflammatory mediator concentration (IL-6 as primary measurable proxy)
- *Controller*: Clinical team (antifibrinolytic continuation/cessation decision)
- *Sensor*: Serial CSF IL-6 measurement via EVD
- *Actuator*: Aminocaproic acid infusion (maintains fibrin clot stability, reduces rebleeding risk)
- *Disturbance inputs*: VCI flow (dilutes sensor readings), AQP4 genotype (alters plant clearance dynamics), bleed grade (determines initial inflammatory load), hydrocephalus (alters CSF compartment topology)
- *Setpoint*: Unknown — no validated IL-6 threshold for DCI prediction currently exists

**The fundamental problem with fixed-duration protocols** is that they implement open-loop control: the actuator (aminocaproic acid) is applied for a fixed period regardless of sensor feedback. Open-loop control is appropriate when: (a) the plant dynamics are uniform across all patients, (b) the disturbance inputs are negligible, and (c) the setpoint is time-invariant. None of these conditions hold in aneurysmal SAH.

**The biomarker-guided protocol** implements closed-loop control: sensor feedback (IL-6 trajectory) modulates actuator output (antifibrinolytic duration). Closed-loop control is appropriate when sensor accuracy is validated, feedback delay is acceptable, and the sensor-to-actuator relationship is characterized. None of these conditions have been formally established — which is precisely why the systematic review described in the query does not yet exist.

**The key insight** is that the systematic review cannot be designed, executed, or interpreted without first characterizing two confounders that have not been formally addressed in the literature: (1) VCI flow rate as a CSF IL-6 dilution artifact, and (2) AQP4 genotype as a clearance-rate modifier. These are not minor covariates — they are parameters that determine whether the sensor (IL-6 measurement) is measuring what it purports to measure.
