This research document investigates three intersecting questions at the frontier of physiological complexity science: (1) Do ventral vagal states show measurably deeper attractor basin geometry compared to sympathetic or dorsal vagal states, as indexed by multiscale entropy (MSE) of heart rate variability (HRV)? (2) In Friston's predictive processing / active inference framework, is social safety or homeostatic prediction the 'deeper prior' in the interoceptive hierarchy? ## Evidence Review

### 1. The Felt Sense Polyvagal Model (WS4-SP-Regulation)

The single most directly relevant entry is the Felt Sense Polyvagal Model, which integrates Gendlin's Focusing (somatic felt sense, interoceptive) with Porges' Polyvagal Theory (autonomic state, neuroceptive). The Six F's framework — Flight/Fight, Fold/Collapse, Flock, Fun/Fired Up, Flow, Fixate/Freeze — maps onto polyvagal states with phenomenological texture.

The critical observation is that 'Flow' (the highest-functioning ventral vagal expression) is characterized by present-moment openness, low threat-appraisal, flexible response range, and felt somatic ease. This is precisely what attractor basin theory would predict for a WIDE, DEEP basin: a system that can be perturbed in many directions and return to equilibrium without catastrophic transition. Freeze and Fold/Collapse, by contrast, suggest narrow, high-walled basins — rigid states that are hard to exit once entered.

The model's Tier 2 status means it is a well-reasoned clinical synthesis, not a controlled experimental finding. The MSE predictions it implies require empirical testing.

### 2. The Murine Gut Atlas and Deep Interoceptive Priors (GRK-reception)

This Tier 1 study maps receptor expression across millions of gut cells under specific-pathogen-free vs. germ-free conditions, revealing that the gut's receptor landscape is microbiota-dependent, spatially organized, and exquisitely tuned to detect molecular signals from diet, host, and microbiota.

For the Friston question, this is highly relevant: if the gut is the primary organ of interoceptive prior construction, and if that prior is microbiota-modulated, then the 'deepest prior' in the interoceptive hierarchy is not cortically generated but gut-generated — transmitted upward through vagal afferents to the nucleus tractus solitarius, the brainstem, and ultimately the cortical generative model. This is consistent with Friston's hierarchical model where lower-level, faster, less abstract priors constrain higher-level, slower, more abstract predictions.

The implication: social safety (a cortical/allostatic prior) operates ON TOP OF a homeostatic prior that is fundamentally gut-vagal. Whether social safety can override or reshape this prior is the key question.

### 3. Hemispheric Entropy Gradient (McGilchrist, WS2-IM entries)

Two entries on left hemisphere function describe its characteristic 'map-like worldview': low entropy, high compression, fragmented, focused on essential information only. This mode discards contextual richness, relational texture, and what McGilchrist calls the 'living' quality of the world.

In information-theoretic terms, this is a LOW ENTROPY state in the sense of low complexity — not thermodynamically disordered, but informationally impoverished. The right hemisphere's mode (contextual, relational, embodied, tolerant of ambiguity) is HIGH ENTROPY in the positive sense — complexity-preserving, flexible, context-sensitive.

This maps structurally onto the polyvagal hierarchy: sympathetic/dorsal states narrow the organism's processing to threat-relevant channels (left hemisphere dominance, low complexity), while ventral vagal states expand processing to include relational, contextual, somatic channels (right hemisphere + ventral vagal co-activation, high complexity). This cross-domain parallel is suggestive but requires empirical bridge-building.

### 4. Accumulation, Elimination, and the Thermodynamic Stakes (WS2-ELIM)

The cellular recycling entry frames aging as an accumulation problem — the failure of elimination systems to keep pace with damage product generation. The thermodynamic trajectory is entropy increase in the NEGATIVE sense: disorder-as-damage rather than disorder-as-flexibility.

This introduces a crucial distinction for the MSE/HRV question: multiscale entropy in the COSTA sense (healthy complexity) is entropy-as-flexibility — the system can explore more of its phase space, has more degrees of freedom, responds adaptively to perturbation. This is DIFFERENT from entropy-as-damage (accumulation of cellular debris, metabolic waste, stochastic molecular errors).

Ventral vagal states likely show HIGH entropy-as-flexibility (wide attractor basin, high MSE) AND LOW entropy-as-damage (better cellular maintenance, lower allostatic load, more efficient elimination). These move in opposite directions — which is precisely what healthy aging theory predicts: complexity-preserving physiology maintains low allostatic load.

### 5. Soul-Level Phenomenology: Phase Transitions and Basin Geometry (PL-SOUL-Restoration)

The soul restoration entry describes worldview reconstruction after shattering loss — 'not a repaired version of the old worldview but a new one that includes the reality of loss as structural material.' This is phenomenologically precise language for what attractor theory calls a BASIN TRANSITION: the old basin (organized around pre-loss assumptions) is destroyed; a new basin (incorporating loss as structural material) forms with different geometry.

Jung's description of the ego-Self axis reconstellating after disruption suggests that post-traumatic growth involves discovering (or constructing) a wider, more inclusive attractor — one that can hold more of reality within its basin. The implication for polyvagal theory: dorsal vagal collapse (freeze) may be the organism's narrowest basin state — the physiological correlate of worldview-shattering — while the recovery arc toward ventral vagal co-regulation mirrors the phenomenological reconstruction of a wider meaning-basin.

### 6. Cross-Scale Operational Homologs (Mirror Entries)

The fractal mirror entries for acetazolamide and aclidinium reveal a striking cross-scale pattern:

- **Acetazolamide (soul mirror)**: 'The habitual, automatic reclaiming of what should have been released, the unconscious recycling of grievance, pressure, or self-concept back into the system before it can be excreted' — this is a description of maladaptive prediction error recycling, the psyche treating its own outputs as inputs in a closed loop. In polyvagal terms: the dorsal vagal state that keeps reabsorbing its own immobilization signal.

- **Aclidinium (soul mirror)**: 'Blocking the specific receptor that converts ambient relational presence into threat-driven contraction' — this is a precise description of what safe therapeutic relationships do in trauma treatment: reducing the gain on the threat-prediction channel without numbing sensation entirely. These metaphors are not evidence — but they point toward a coherent mechanistic story: healthy ventral vagal states are characterized by REDUCED PRECISION-WEIGHTING of threat-related prediction channels, allowing wider sampling of environmental/relational signals, which increases the effective degrees of freedom of the autonomic system, which registers as higher MSE.

## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A (Conservative, Tier 1)

**Claim**: Ventral vagal states show measurably greater multiscale entropy across multiple timescales compared to sympathetic or dorsal vagal states, reflecting wider attractor basin geometry.

This is the most parsimonious hypothesis, consistent with the well-established complexity-loss literature in physiological time series. The known facts are: (a) HRV decreases with age, disease, and chronic stress; (b) MSE of HRV tracks this decline and provides additional discriminatory power beyond power spectral analysis; (c) polyvagal theory maps autonomic states onto a flexibility hierarchy. The synthetic prediction — that MSE directly tracks the polyvagal ladder — has face validity and is testable.

The key gap: the polyvagal 'flavor' of HRV has not been systematically distinguished from HRV magnitude in MSE analyses. An athlete in sympathetic arousal may show high HRV and potentially high MSE despite NOT being in a ventral vagal social engagement state. Disambiguation requires multimodal measurement (HRV + facial affect + subjective felt sense + context).

### Hypothesis B (Integrative, Tier 2)

**Claim**: Homeostatic prediction is the deeper Fristonian prior; social safety achieves complexity expansion by modulating that homeostatic prior's precision-weighting through the gut-vagal axis.

This hypothesis attempts to adjudicate the 'deeper prior' question by placing it in the phylogenetic and developmental sequence: gut-vagal homeostatic signaling precedes social engagement circuitry evolutionarily (Porges' phylogenetic hypothesis) and develops earlier in infancy (visceral regulation before social referencing). In Friston's hierarchy, priors that are faster, lower, and more ancient constrain slower, higher, more recent priors.

BUT: Friston's own framework may not support a simple depth-by-phylogeny mapping. The hierarchy is defined by abstraction level and timescale, not evolutionary age. Social priors (attachment models) operate on LONGER timescales (months/years) than homeostatic priors (seconds/minutes), which in predictive processing terms may make them HIGHER in the hierarchy and thus more fundamental in a different sense — providing the hyperpriors that give meaning to homeostatic signals.

This ambiguity is unresolvable without direct access to Friston's interoceptive hierarchy papers, which are not in the corpus.

### Hypothesis C (Radical, Tier 3)

**Claim**: The attractor basin geometry of the nervous system is the physiological analog of what contemplative and depth-psychological traditions call the 'soul's capacity' — its range of experience, tolerance for paradox, and resilience to dissolution. Individuation, in Jungian terms, is literally the expansion of attractor basin depth.

This is the most speculative hypothesis, but it makes a specific empirical prediction: longitudinal studies of Jungian analysis, psychedelic therapy, or depth-psychological intervention should show measurable increases in HRV MSE over the course of successful treatment — with MSE increases correlating with qualitative measures of worldview expansion and increased tolerance for ambiguity.

The hypothesis is partially falsifiable and points toward an emerging literature on psychedelic therapy + HRV that has not been systematically analyzed through the MSE lens.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection is measurement confounding: HRV magnitude and MSE are not independent, and the conditions that produce high HRV (parasympathetic dominance, low sympathetic tone, rest) do not cleanly map onto 'ventral vagal social engagement.' Polyvagal theory predicts that ventral vagal states are characterized by active parasympathetic engagement WITH SIMULTANEOUS mild sympathetic arousal (the 'play' state), not simply maximal parasympathetic dominance. This means the HRV pattern of ventral vagal states may be different from simple high-RMSSD rest states, and the MSE profile may be correspondingly complex.

Furthermore, dorsal vagal immobilization (freeze) may produce paradoxically HIGH entropy in the sense of DISORGANIZED variability — not the structured complexity of healthy HRV, but the irregular, unpatterned fluctuation of a destabilized system. If MSE cannot distinguish 'healthy complexity' from 'disorganized variability,' the hypothesis may hold numerically while being mechanistically misleading.

### Against Hypothesis B

The gut-vagal-as-deepest-prior claim faces the objection that the AMYGDALA, not the gut, is the primary prior for threat detection in mammals — and the amygdala's threat priors develop through SOCIAL experience (attachment, early relational trauma), not through microbiome composition. If social priors are instantiated in amygdalar circuits that develop BEFORE gut-vagal toning is complete (which is arguable), then social safety may be the deeper prior in the developmental and computational sense.

Additionally, the murine gut atlas, while Tier 1, is not directly about HRV or polyvagal states — it is about receptor expression in the GI tract. The inferential leap from 'gut receptor diversity' to 'deep homeostatic prior in Friston's hierarchy' is several steps beyond the data.

### Against Hypothesis C

The primary objection is the level-of-description problem: consciousness (soul, spirit) and autonomic nervous system physiology operate at different levels of description, and the 'attractor basin' metaphor applied to worldview-geometry may be just that — a metaphor, not a homology. The fact that the same geometric language applies at multiple levels does not mean the same MECHANISM operates at multiple levels.

Jung's individuation and Friston's free energy minimization share surface structural similarities (both involve 'reducing surprise,' both involve expanding the generative model) but emerge from incommensurable theoretical traditions. Conflating them risks a sophisticated category error.

## Synthesis

The most defensible evolved insight is a tiered model that preserves the strengths of each hypothesis while acknowledging the limits of the available evidence:

**Tier 1 (High confidence)**: Multiscale entropy of HRV provides a richer window into autonomic attractor geometry than raw HRV power, and the complexity-loss hypothesis predicts that pathological autonomic states (chronic sympathetic dominance, dorsal vagal freeze, trauma) will show reduced MSE. This is well-established.

**Tier 2 (Medium confidence)**: Social safety achieves its autonomic flexibility effect by modulating homeostatic prediction precision at the gut-vagal interface — not by operating as a cortical override but by recalibrating the ascending interoceptive signal. The Felt Sense Polyvagal Model's integration of somatic felt sense and social engagement provides clinical support for this mechanism, though mechanistic confirmation requires Tier 1 neurogastroenterology + HRV + MSE studies.

**Tier 3 (Low confidence, generative)**: The phenomenological experience of 'safety' — at soul level — may have a measurable autonomic signature in HRV MSE, and longitudinal depth-psychological work may produce quantifiable increases in autonomic complexity. This is worth investigating but is currently below the evidence threshold for confident assertion.

**On the 'deeper prior' question**: The homeostatic prior is likely deeper in the phylogenetic and subcortical sense; social safety is likely deeper in the developmental-meaning sense. These are different claims about different dimensions of 'depth.' The most intellectually honest answer is that Friston's framework does not cleanly resolve this without specifying what 'depth' means in the hierarchy — timescale, abstraction level, evolutionary age, or computational generativity.

## Implications

### For Clinical Practice

If ventral vagal states show genuinely higher MSE (wider attractor basins), then therapeutic interventions should aim not just to INCREASE HRV power but to INCREASE HRV complexity — which requires interventions that promote variability and flexibility, not just parasympathetic dominance. Deep slow breathing (which maximizes HRV power at 0.1 Hz) may produce high-power but low-complexity HRV — a NARROW attractor with high amplitude. What is needed for genuine ventral vagal basin geometry may be interventions that produce SPONTANEOUS, UNFORCED variability — play, authentic social engagement, creative improvisation, flow states.

### For the Gut-Brain Interface

If the gut-vagal axis carries the deep homeostatic prior, then interventions targeting the microbiome (diet, probiotics, fermented foods, fecal transplant) may have measurable effects on HRV MSE — and this effect may be partially independent of social/psychological interventions. The murine data suggests this is plausible; human longitudinal studies with simultaneous microbiome and HRV MSE measurement would be decisive.

### For Soul-Level Integration

The phenomenological claim — that worldview reconstruction after trauma involves attractor basin expansion — generates a testable prediction: the temporal arc of post-traumatic growth (as measured by Tedeschi & Calhoun's PTGI) should correlate with longitudinal HRV MSE increase. If soul-level basin geometry and autonomic basin geometry are genuinely coupled (not merely metaphorically parallel), this correlation should be detectable in the 18-24 month window after major loss.
