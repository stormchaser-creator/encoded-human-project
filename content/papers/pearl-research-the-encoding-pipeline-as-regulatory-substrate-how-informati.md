### 4. Trauma as Pipeline Reception Blockage

The van der Kolk entry (WS2-BK-Reception) describes a specific disruption pattern: impaired capacity to receive positive relational input post-trauma. In pipeline terms, this is a reception failure — the input stage of the soul-level encoding pipeline is compromised, meaning that even when facilitation and convergence conditions are met (safe environment, caring relationship), the system cannot derive a stable representation of safety from the input. ## Debate

### Against A
The hypothalamus is recursive and feedback-heavy, not a simple directed pipeline. The 'encoding pipeline' metaphor may oversimplify a network topology that is better described by attractor dynamics than by sequential processing stages. However, the functional stages (receive, encode, derive, facilitate, converge) remain identifiable even in recursive systems — they describe temporal phases of processing, not necessarily anatomically segregated modules.

### Against B
The soul-level 'encoding pipeline' is compelling but the measurement problem is serious. What instruments assess 'reception capacity' at the psychological level? Attachment security scales (AAI, ECR) offer partial measurement, but they do not decompose into pipeline stages in a validated way. The fractal claim risks being unfalsifiable in practice.

### Against C
The trans-density architecture claim currently lacks a shared measurement framework. Using phenomenological convergence reports as evidence for a unified pipeline is methodologically weak. However, the convergent phenomenology of substrate substitution interventions across densities is a genuine empirical anomaly that deserves investigation rather than dismissal.

## Implications

1. **For biological intervention:** Target the specific pipeline stage that is disrupted, not the symptom. Metabolic interventions (sleep, ketones, hormonal balance) address the substrate layer. Neuroendocrine interventions (EPA, estrogen management) address the facilitation and convergence stages.

2. **For psychological intervention:** Trauma treatment must restore reception capacity before attempting derivation or facilitation-stage interventions (cognitive reprocessing, behavioral activation). Somatic approaches that restore interoceptive access are not preparatory — they are the first essential pipeline stage.

3. **For contemplative practice:** The practices that most directly address spirit-level encoding pipeline function are those that work at the reception stage — open awareness, resting attention, non-reactive witnessing — rather than practices that intervene at derivation (analysis, inquiry) or facilitation (devotion, aspiration). The sequence matters.

4. **For research design:** Studies investigating any of these interventions would benefit from measuring outcomes across pipeline stages and densities, not just at the convergence endpoint (symptom reduction, wellbeing scores).
