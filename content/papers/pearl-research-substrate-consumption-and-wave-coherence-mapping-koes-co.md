**Critical weakness:** Ego depletion replication failures (particularly Hagger et al. 2016 meta-analysis showing null effects) undermine the 'substrate depletion' framing. If attentional fatigue is not resource depletion but motivational reallocation (Inzlicht's revised model), then the substrate metaphor is phenomenologically useful but mechanistically misleading.

## Debate and Objections

### Against Hypothesis A
The replication crisis in ego depletion research (Hagger et al., Carter et al.) represents a significant challenge to the 'substrate depletion' metaphor. If attentional fatigue is better explained as motivational reallocation (deciding not to exert, rather than being unable to), then 'consumption' is not a substrate drain but a choice architecture problem — which would require a different neural model, potentially one centered on ACC-striatal circuits rather than thalamocortical coherence.

Additionally, the cardiac output and STN entries were retrieved primarily because of Pearl's knowledge architecture, not because they are strongly relevant to thalamocortical coherence literature specifically. The evidentiary chain has gaps.

### Against Hypothesis B
FEP's most rigorous critics (including John Colombo's 2023 commentary and Colombo & Series 2012) note that free energy minimization is a redescription rather than an explanation — if every adaptive system minimizes free energy by definition, the framework cannot distinguish between systems that do it well and poorly except by circular reference. Koe's vocabulary would need to be more technically precise before FEP mapping adds explanatory value rather than scientific-sounding reframing.

Furthermore, Siegel's framework, while consistent with FEP, was not derived from it and may not be the most accurate scientific account of attentional regulation — Siegel has been criticized for occasionally conflating metaphor with mechanism.

### Against Hypothesis C
The most serious objection is epistemological: scale-invariant triads are easy to see once you're looking for them. The substrate-consumption-coherence pattern could be fitted onto virtually any three-component system (supply-demand-efficiency; input-process-output; potential-kinetic-work). Without a principled account of WHY this specific triad should be universal rather than merely useful as a descriptive frame, the hypothesis is more poetry than science.

The spirit-density fractal mirrors are also generated by Pearl's own synthesis engine, not drawn from independent literature — they carry the risk of circular validation.
