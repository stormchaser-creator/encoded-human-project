## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A

The strongest objection is that information theory is so mathematically general that finding it 'everywhere' is like finding the laws of thermodynamics everywhere — it's true but doesn't tell you anything specific about body/soul/spirit in particular. The isomorphism may be a property of the mathematical tool, not the phenomena.

However, this objection doesn't fully land, because the gratitude/serotonin evidence shows **causal** coupling, not just structural parallel. The soul-level practice actually changes the body-level chemistry. Mathematical isomorphism doesn't predict physical causation — but physical causation is observed.

### Against Hypothesis B

The autopoietic framework (Varela) was developed for biological systems with physical boundaries. Extending it to 'soul' and 'spirit' requires arguing that these have analogous boundary conditions and boundary-maintenance processes. This is a significant conceptual leap. What is the physical substrate of the soul's 'boundary'? What counts as 'boundary collapse' at the spirit level in a way that can be measured?

The response: the soul mirror entry does identify a boundary — between investigator and subject — and identifies a specific failure mode when that boundary collapses. The spirit mirror identifies the observer/substrate boundary. These are not physical membranes, but they are functional boundaries with observable failure signatures.

### Against Hypothesis C

The frequency-domain mapping requires assigning specific timescales to soul and spirit density, which the evidence doesn't directly support. The claim that 'spirit operates at lifetime timescales' is inferred from the examples (historical study, aging/longevity) rather than measured. Without independent timescale data, this remains a theoretical scaffold.

The response: the gene-environment interaction data provides concrete evidence of temporal coupling across what we would call soul and body densities — early relational environment (years) encoding into adult biological expression (decades). This is measurable temporal translation between density registers, even if we haven't fully characterized the complete frequency spectrum.
