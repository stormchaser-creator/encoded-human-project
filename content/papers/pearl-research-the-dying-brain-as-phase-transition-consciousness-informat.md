## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: The Neurochemical Artifact Model

**Claim**: NDEs during cardiac arrest are neurochemical and electrophysiological artifacts of acute hypoxic-ischemic stress. The Borjigin gamma surge represents terminal depolarization waves — pathological, not functional, neural activity. EEG signatures are noise produced by dying tissue, not organized consciousness. NDE phenomenology (tunnel, light, life review, deceased relatives) reflects the stereotyped output of specific neurotransmitter systems under extreme metabolic stress: endogenous N,N-Dimethyltryptamine (DMT) release, glutamate excitotoxic cascade, endogenous opioid surge, and default mode network disinhibition.

**Evidence Support**: The Tier 1 establishment that neurotransmitter modulation explains all pharmacologically-induced altered states is the backbone of this hypothesis. The phenomenological overlap between ketamine dissociation, deep meditation, hypoxia-induced euphoria, and NDE reports supports a common neurotransmitter mechanism. The AWARE II finding that only 2% of survivors with meaningful EEG data reported NDEs suggests that NDEs are not reliably correlated with cardiac arrest per se, but with specific neurochemical conditions that vary by patient.

**Lenses**: This hypothesis invokes signal_processing (the NDE as terminal noise artifact), control_theory (loss of homeostatic control producing runaway excitation), and information_theory (gamma 'signal' as high-amplitude noise rather than organized information).

**Falsifiable by**: Verified out-of-body perceptions of specific hidden visual targets in patients with confirmed EEG flatline AND confirmed absence of cerebral blood flow. If such verification occurs under rigorous methodology, the neurochemical artifact model must be substantially revised.

### Hypothesis B: The Phase Transition / Criticality Model

**Claim**: The dying brain undergoes a genuine phase transition from a metabolically-constrained, damped-oscillatory regime to a briefly unconstrained, near-critical state where network synchrony and information integration transiently maximize before permanent collapse. This is the same criticality that normal waking consciousness occupies — the brain operating near the edge of a phase transition between ordered and chaotic dynamics — but metabolic removal of damping mechanisms allows a final, brief excursion to a high-complexity state. Terminal lucidity represents this same phenomenon in the context of neurodegenerative disease: metabolic failure removing the 'noise floor' that was suppressing residual network activity.

**Evidence Support**: Borjigin's gamma coherence data is specifically consistent with phase transition dynamics — the simultaneous surge across multiple frequency bands (gamma, beta, alpha) suggests global network synchronization rather than local depolarization waves. Criticality theory in neuroscience (Beggs & Plenz, Chialvo) establishes that healthy brains operate near critical points. The secondary brain damage / oxidative metabolism entry supports the idea that ischemic cascades produce non-linear dynamics. The circadian coupled-oscillator framing suggests that neural systems losing metabolic support will undergo characteristic synchronization events before permanent desynchronization.

**Lenses**: phase_transitions (primary), complexity_emergence (the NDE as emergent property of critical state), coupled_oscillators (final phase-locking before desynchronization), topology_morphogenesis (the shape of the dying brain's information processing).

**Falsifiable by**: If Lempel-Ziv complexity and phi calculations on peri-arrest EEG show monotonic DECREASE throughout the dying process (never a surge), criticality hypothesis is falsified. Also falsified if terminal lucidity cases systematically occur only in patients with preserved structural islands visible on MRI.

### Hypothesis C: The Filter Dissolution Model

**Claim**: Consciousness is not generated by neural activity but is the background field within which neural activity occurs. The brain normally functions as a reducing valve (Bergson, 1896; Huxley, 1954; Fenwick, 2008) — constraining the total field of possible consciousness to the particular self-model useful for biological survival. Terminal neural activity, including Borjigin gamma surges, does not generate NDE experience but reflects the dissolution of this filtering function, releasing consciousness from its normal constraints. IIT's phi metric, properly interpreted, suggests that phi is a measure of how much intrinsic causal power a system has — which in a filter theory becomes a measure of constraint capacity, not generation capacity.

**Evidence Support**: This is the most speculative hypothesis and has the least direct evidence. Its support comes from the philosophical coherence of the filter model with IIT's mathematics (though Tononi himself does not endorse a filter interpretation), terminal lucidity cases verified at autopsy as having destroyed neural substrates, and the fractal mirror evidence that 'consciousness is self-regulating by nature' and that 'coherence arises from deeper intrinsic order preceding all imposed structure.'

**Lenses**: em_fields (consciousness as field phenomenon), fractals (the filter pattern repeating at cell, organism, and metaphysical scales), network_theory (consciousness as network property, not node property), information_theory (the brain as bandwidth-limiter, not information source).

**Falsifiable by**: Discovery of consciousness (verified NDE with accurate out-of-body perceptions) in patients with ZERO detected neural activity by any available method, would support this hypothesis. Alternatively, if every verified terminal lucidity case shows preserved structural substrate on imaging, the filter hypothesis loses its strongest empirical anchor.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A
The neurochemical artifact model's greatest vulnerability is terminal lucidity. Patients with end-stage Alzheimer's disease — verified at autopsy to have extensive cortical atrophy, neurofibrillary tangle burden, and amyloid plaques throughout memory and language regions — occasionally hold coherent conversations, recognize family members, and express emotional nuance in the hours before death. If the substrate for these functions is structurally destroyed, the neurochemical hypothesis cannot easily invoke 'remaining neurotransmitter systems' as the explanation. This is the datum that Hypothesis A must either explain or concede.

### Against Hypothesis B
The criticality hypothesis risks being a sophisticated re-labeling: calling the terminal gamma surge a 'phase transition to high complexity' rather than 'terminal depolarization wave' may be post-hoc interpretation. The specific mathematical claim — that phi increases during the dying process — has not been computed from real peri-arrest EEG data. Phase transitions in physical systems are defined by specific mathematical signatures (power-law scaling, diverging correlation length, universal critical exponents). Whether the dying brain satisfies these criteria is entirely unknown. The hypothesis is elegant but empirically untested.

### Against Hypothesis C
The filter theory faces the explanatory specificity problem: if NDEs are releases from neural constraint, why are they not maximally diverse — every person experiencing unique, unconstrained phenomenology? Instead, NDEs show remarkable cross-cultural consistency: tunnel experiences, light at the end, life review, encountering deceased relatives, border/boundary experience. This consistency is better explained by universal human neural architecture generating stereotyped responses to universal hypoxic conditions than by a universal filter releasing a common background consciousness. Furthermore, the filter theory is effectively unfalsifiable: any absence of neural correlate can be attributed to insufficient measurement technology.

## Synthesis

The most defensible position integrates elements of Hypotheses A and B while holding C as a genuine philosophical possibility that current methodology cannot exclude:

**Core synthesis**: The dying brain undergoes a real electrophysiological event (consistent with Borjigin data) that is neurochemically mediated (consistent with Hypothesis A) and may represent a genuine phase transition to a transiently high-complexity state (consistent with Hypothesis B). This event produces rich subjective experience via standard neural mechanisms — the same mechanisms that make ketamine and DMT produce NDE-like states. The experience is therefore 'real' as subjective experience, neurochemically generated, and potentially at a complexity peak — but does not require non-physical consciousness.

Terminal lucidity remains the most anomalous datum. The most parsimonious explanation within Hypothesis B is that metabolic disinhibition releases activity from structurally preserved but functionally suppressed neural islands — and that autopsy findings of extensive damage do not rule out microscale preservation sufficient for the observed behavior. This is testable with ultra-high-field MRI and post-mortem histology in future terminal lucidity cases.

IIT's phi metric, if it can be computed on peri-arrest EEG data, would provide the most decisive test of whether the dying brain's gamma surge represents organized or disorganized information processing. This computation has not yet been performed with the necessary rigor.

## Implications

**For Resuscitation Medicine**: If the dying brain reliably produces a brief high-complexity state, resuscitation medicine faces an unexpected ethical consideration: the transition from life to death may involve a phenomenologically rich period that current protocols ignore. The timing of resuscitation attempts relative to this period may matter subjectively even if it doesn't matter for survival outcome.

**For IIT**: If phi can be shown to transiently increase during cardiac arrest, this would be a striking confirmation of IIT's predictions and would make the theory far more empirically tractable. If phi shows no increase, it suggests that the gamma surge is indeed noise, which would also be informative.

**For the Soul/Spirit Densities**: The fractal mirrors in Pearl's knowledge base consistently map the 'coherence without imposed constraint' pattern onto deeper questions about the nature of self and awareness. The dying brain question is not merely a neuroscience question — it is the question of whether awareness is a product of complexity or the condition for complexity. This remains genuinely open.
