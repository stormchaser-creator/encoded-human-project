## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Structural Isomorphism Between Vritti Taxonomy and Predictive Processing Components

The Yoga Sutras enumerate five types of vritti (mental fluctuations): pramana (valid cognition), viparyaya (erroneous cognition), vikalpa (conceptualization), nidra (sleep/absence), and smriti (memory). These map onto predictive processing components as follows:

- **Pramana** → successful prediction: prior and likelihood converge, posterior is confident
- **Viparyaya** → prediction error with incorrect model update: the prior is updated in the wrong direction
- **Vikalpa** → model-based cognition decoupled from sensory evidence: pure generative model operation
- **Nidra** → predictive processing in the absence of sensory evidence: default mode network dynamics
- **Smriti** → retrieval of stored priors: activation of cached generative models

The practice of **nirodha** (cessation) maps onto the progressive suppression of precision-weighted prediction errors — the meditating mind reduces the gain on bottom-up sensory signals, allowing top-down priors to become quiescent rather than actively predicting.

**Falsification**: If this mapping produces no novel predictions about neural correlates of vritti-specific mental states that differ from predictions made by other cognitive frameworks, the isomorphism is merely redescriptive.

### Hypothesis B: Kosha Model as Nested Markov Blankets

The five koshas (annamaya, pranamaya, manomaya, vijnanamaya, anandamaya) can be understood as nested levels of biological self-organization, each maintaining its own Markov blanket (statistical boundary separating internal from external states) while passing compressed predictions to higher levels:

- **Annamaya kosha** (physical body) → cellular/organismal Markov blanket: metabolic homeostasis
- **Pranamaya kosha** (energetic body) → mitochondrial/autonomic Markov blanket: HRV, respiratory rhythms, bioelectric fields
- **Manomaya kosha** (mental body) → cortical Markov blanket: sensory prediction and motor control
- **Vijnanamaya kosha** (wisdom body) → prefrontal/metacognitive Markov blanket: model selection and precision weighting
- **Anandamaya kosha** (bliss body) → perhaps: the prior distribution itself, the 'shape' of the generative model that determines what kind of experiences are possible

The endosymbiotic mitochondria evidence grounds the pranamaya layer concretely: mitochondria are semi-autonomous predictive systems that regulate cellular energy budgets — the 'energy body' is not mystical but is the biological layer of cellular energetics operating with semi-alien autonomy.

**Falsification**: If advanced practitioners show no distinct physiological signatures at multiple biological scales simultaneously (HRV, EEG gamma, metabolic markers, inflammatory profiles), the nested architecture claim is not empirically supported.

### Hypothesis C: Purusa as the Irreducible Observer — A Formally Precise Hard Problem

The Yoga Sutras' most radical claim is ontological: purusa (pure witnessing consciousness) is categorically distinct from prakriti (matter-energy-mind, including cognitive processes). This is not substance dualism in the Cartesian sense — it is more precisely a claim about **reference frames**: the observing frame cannot be fully described within the system it observes.

This maps onto:
- **Gödel's incompleteness**: no sufficiently complex formal system can prove its own consistency from within
- **The measurement problem in quantum mechanics**: the act of observation cannot be fully included in the system being observed without generating paradox
- **The hard problem of consciousness**: why there is 'something it is like' to be a predictive processing system — which the formalism itself cannot explain

The fractal structure of the endosymbiotic argument is relevant: at each scale, there is an 'observer' (the outer membrane selecting what passes through) that cannot be fully absorbed into the system it contains. The mitochondrion has a perspective (its own genome, its own telos) that was never fully domesticated by the eukaryotic cell. This is not metaphor — it is a biological instance of irreducible perspectival structure.

**Falsification**: If IIT or global workspace theory successfully accounts for all phenomenological properties attributed to purusa (non-temporal, non-local, contentless awareness) without positing an additional ontological category, the Yoga Sutras' claim reduces to a sophisticated but ultimately eliminable phenomenological vocabulary.

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A
The most serious objection is that predictive processing is so general it can absorb almost any systematic cognitive framework. This makes the isomorphism unfalsifiable in practice. However, the specific structural mapping between vritti types and predictive processing components generates concrete, novel predictions about neural correlates that could be tested with high-density EEG in experienced practitioners — this partially addresses the concern.

### Against Hypothesis B
The kosha model was developed experientially, not theoretically, and its five-layer structure may reflect phenomenological salience rather than biological necessity. Why five layers? The Free Energy Principle predicts as many Markov blanket levels as there are distinct scales of self-organization — which could be many more or fewer than five. The mapping may be fitting five onto an arbitrary number. However, the five-layer structure may reflect the natural grain of human experiential access to these layers, which is itself a phenomenologically interesting fact.

### Against Hypothesis C
This hypothesis risks being unfalsifiable in principle — if the observer is by definition outside the system, no experiment conducted within the system can falsify the claim. However, this is precisely the structure of the hard problem of consciousness, and the Yoga Sutras' articulation of it may be more formally precise than commonly recognized. The risk of category error (conflating biological boundary-maintenance with first-person phenomenological perspective) is real and must be acknowledged.

## Synthesis

The most defensible synthesis holds three claims simultaneously:

1. **The isomorphism is structurally real**: The Yoga Sutras were developed through systematic, empirical (first-person) investigation of consciousness, and their taxonomic structure anticipates predictive processing not because of historical connection but because both are maps of the same territory — the functional architecture of mind. This is analogous to how ancient Chinese medicine's description of meridians may (partially) map onto fascial planes and autonomic nerve distributions — not because ancient physicians had anatomy textbooks, but because sustained systematic observation of the same system converges on its real structure.

2. **The biological evidence grounds the middle layers**: Endosymbiotic mitochondria, nested metabolic memory, and multi-scale biomarker architectures demonstrate that the 'energetic' and 'cognitive' layers of biological organization are not metaphysical postulates but real levels of self-organizing dynamics, each with semi-autonomous predictive properties.

3. **The observer problem remains genuinely open**: Neither predictive processing nor any other computational framework has resolved the hard problem of consciousness. The Yoga Sutras' purusa/prakriti distinction may encode a formally precise version of this problem that deserves engagement from philosophers of mind and consciousness scientists — not as mysticism but as a sophisticated phenomenological hypothesis.
