This is the definition of biological hysteresis: the current state of the system depends on its history, not merely on its current inputs. The relevance to wound healing is direct: if environmental insults leave methylation signatures, mechanical trauma and local inflammation should also leave epigenetic marks at wound sites and in circulating immune cells that were activated during the injury response.

**Extracellular matrix as structural memory (decoded-e9b76a44897a):** Twenty-six co-regulated aging modules were identified across tissues, with extracellular matrix organization appearing alongside inflammatory response and mitochondrial function as a core phenotypic cluster. These alterations are not merely cosmetic: stiffer matrices transmit mechanical forces differently to resident cells via integrin-mediated mechanotransduction, alter growth factor sequestration (VEGF, TGF-β, and others are stored in ECM and released by stiffness-dependent mechanisms), and bias macrophage phenotype toward pro-inflammatory states. The co-regulation of ECM with inflammation means that inflammatory history and structural history are mechanistically entangled: prior inflammation leaves ECM permanently altered, and that altered ECM biases future inflammatory responses.

If depletion is severe or repeated, it may establish persistent zones of metabolic insufficiency that impair future repair capacity. The tissue-specific and compartment-specific nature of NAD+ regulation means that local wound-site depletion does not necessarily equilibrate with systemic NAD+ levels, creating a persistent local energetic deficit that constitutes a form of metabolic memory.

## Hypothesis Generation

### Hypothesis A: Multi-Substrate Biological Memory (Conservative, Tier 1)

Wound healing hysteresis is mechanistically encoded in at least three overlapping biological substrates:

1. **ECM stiffness and architecture** altered by prior fibrosis, which changes mechanotransduction signals to wound-edge cells and sequesters or releases growth factors differently than naive matrix
2. **Epigenetic methylation signatures** at wound-site stromal cells and at circulating immune cells activated during injury, biasing future inflammatory responses toward the profile established during prior injury
3. **NAD+/redox compartment gradients** in wound-adjacent tissue that reflect the metabolic cost of prior repair and may not fully normalize, impairing mitochondrial function and PARP-dependent repair in re-injury

These three substrates collectively produce a tissue state that is path-dependent: re-injury of previously wounded tissue encounters a mechanically stiffer, epigenetically biased, and metabolically depleted microenvironment — healing differently not because the injury is different but because the tissue is different.

**Analytical lenses:** Control theory (set-point alteration), topology/morphogenesis (ECM architecture as gradient), information theory (epigenetic encoding of inflammatory history), phase transitions (fibrosis as a state that resists return to naive architecture)

**Falsifiable by:** Longitudinal studies showing that epigenetic signatures at healed wound sites return to pre-injury baseline, ECM stiffness normalizes fully, and NAD+ gradients equilibrate — with no difference in re-injury healing outcomes

### Hypothesis B: Coupled Oscillator Hysteresis Spanning Biological and Psychosocial Scales (Integrative, Tier 2)

Wound healing hysteresis is not a tissue-only phenomenon but emerges from coupling between biomechanical, neuroimmune, and psychosocial regulatory systems operating at different timescales. Altered tissue architecture changes pain signaling and sensory afferent patterns, which modulates autonomic tone and HPA axis activity, which shapes systemic inflammatory cytokine levels, which affects social behavior and relational engagement, which feeds back into neuroimmune regulation of wound-site inflammation. A patient's psychological history — particularly relational isolation or trauma — is therefore mechanistically embedded in their tissue's healing trajectory through the neuroimmune coupling pathway.

This means 'tissue memory' in wound healing includes memory encoded in: nervous system sensitization patterns (central sensitization at previously injured sites), HPA axis calibration (altered cortisol rhythms from chronic stress), and immune cell epigenetic programming from prior inflammatory activations — all of which are influenced by psychosocial history and all of which directly affect wound healing.

**Analytical lenses:** Coupled oscillators (biomechanical, neuroimmune, psychosocial frequencies), network theory (hub nodes in HPA-immune-tissue network), fractals (self-similar hysteresis at cell, organ, and psychosocial scales)

**Falsifiable by:** Studies showing no correlation between psychosocial history and wound healing outcomes when controlling for direct physiological variables; or showing that relational interventions produce no measurable change in inflammatory markers at wound sites

### Hypothesis C: Biophotonic Field Encoding of Tissue Memory (Radical, Tier 3)

The ROS landscape and mitochondrial dynamics of wound tissue — which change dramatically during injury and may not fully normalize — produce measurable alterations in ultraweak photon emission (UPE) profiles that persist in healed tissue. These altered UPE profiles represent an electromagnetic signature of prior injury that potentially influences cell behavior through mechanisms not yet characterized (possibly through photobiomodulation of mitochondrial cytochrome c oxidase or through stochastic resonance effects on membrane dynamics). Tissue memory is thus partially encoded in an electromagnetic field layer that overlays and potentially coordinates the biochemical and structural memory layers.

A softer version: even if UPEs are not causally functional, they serve as non-invasive readouts of the integrated metabolic/redox state of previously wounded tissue, allowing non-invasive assessment of 'healing debt.'

**Analytical lenses:** Electromagnetic fields, information theory (UPE as signal encoding tissue state), complexity/emergence (field-level coordination of multi-substrate memory), signal processing (wound tissue as altered signal emitter)

**Falsifiable by:** UPE measurements of matched healed vs. never-injured tissue showing no statistically significant difference; or in vitro studies showing no cell behavioral changes from UPE-level light doses

## Debate

### Against Hypothesis A
The ECM, epigenetic, and NAD+ changes are individually documented but their causal contribution to hysteresis in re-injury specifically is mostly inferred. Most wound healing research measures acute outcomes at single timepoints rather than tracking long-term epigenetic or metabolic changes post-healing. The hypothesis may be identifying co-occurring phenomena rather than mechanistically linked hysteresis. Additionally, many wounds do heal to near-normal function, suggesting the 'memory' can be overcome — which weakens the attractor language.

**Counter:** The distinction between 'can be overcome' and 'not present' matters. Hysteresis doesn't mean irreversibility — it means path-dependence. The fact that more energy input is required to heal re-injured tissue to the same quality IS the hysteresis, even if achievable.

### Against Hypothesis B
The causal chain from ECM stiffness → pain → autonomic tone → HPA activation → inflammatory cytokines → healing impairment is long and each link adds noise. Animal models with controlled social environments still show wound healing variation, suggesting tissue-level factors are sufficient without invoking psychosocial coupling. The coupling may be real but may contribute relatively small effect size compared to local tissue factors.

**Counter:** Effect size in population studies of psychological stress on wound healing is actually substantial (consistently 40-60% slower healing in high-stress conditions in controlled human studies). The coupling is empirically supported even if the mechanism is not fully characterized.

### Against Hypothesis C
Biophotonic emission as informationally functional (rather than merely correlative) in wound healing context is highly speculative. The signal intensities involved are at the edge of measurability and the proposed mechanisms for how UPEs would influence cell behavior against biochemical background noise are not established. This risks being unfalsifiable in practice even if theoretically falsifiable.

**Counter:** The softer version — UPEs as readout rather than driver — is much more defensible and still scientifically useful. Redirecting the hypothesis toward UPE profiling as diagnostic of tissue healing state rather than causative in it makes it both testable and clinically relevant.

## Synthesis

The three hypotheses are not competing so much as nested. Hypothesis A describes the substrate layer — what biological structures encode wound memory. Hypothesis B describes the coupling layer — how those substrates are influenced by and influence systems beyond the wound site. ## Clinical and Research Implications

1. **Chronic wound management:** If hysteresis is real, single-intervention approaches will be systematically underpowered. Addressing only the wound surface without addressing the epigenetic, metabolic, and psychosocial memory layers explains why many chronic wounds resist treatment.

2. **Prehabilitation and wound preparation:** Interventions that reset the tissue attractor state before planned surgery (addressing NAD+ depletion, inflammatory epigenetic priming, relational support) might improve healing outcomes more than post-surgical interventions.

3. **Re-injury risk:** Athletes and patients with prior injuries may carry structural and epigenetic wound memory that elevates re-injury risk and impairs healing — even when imaging shows 'complete' recovery. This has implications for return-to-activity protocols.

4. **Measurement gap:** There is currently no clinical standard for assessing 'healing debt' — the accumulated hysteresis from prior wounds. UPE profiling, methylation arrays of wound-adjacent tissue, or NAD+ ratio measurements might serve this function.

5. **Psychosocial integration:** The neuroimmune coupling evidence makes relational support and stress management evidence-based components of wound care, not adjuncts.
