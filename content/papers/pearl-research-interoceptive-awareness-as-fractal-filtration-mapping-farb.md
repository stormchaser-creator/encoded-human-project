## Debate

### Hypothesis A

**Strongest Support**: PPI was the dominant task-based connectivity method in affective neuroscience circa 2013. Farb's group has consistently used insula-seeded analyses. MAIA-2 improving on MAIA-1 fit indices is standard for psychometric revision studies.

**Strongest Objection**: Without the actual paper, any specific methodological claim risks being precisely wrong in ways that matter. Farb 2013 is cited as a key paper in interoception literature — it may have used dynamic causal modeling (DCM) to establish directionality of connectivity, which would be methodologically distinct from PPI. The preprocessing pipeline details cannot be inferred from field conventions alone.

**Unexamined Assumption**: That 2013 neuroimaging practice was homogeneous. In reality, Farb's group was methodologically innovative; they may have used granger causality or effective connectivity methods uncommon at the time.

### Hypothesis B

**Strongest Support**: The fractal renal filtration pattern across three densities demonstrates that Pearl's triadic framework produces coherent, non-redundant distinctions. Garfinkel's multi-register taxonomy of interoception provides independent empirical support for the claim that interoceptive awareness is not unidimensional. **Strongest Objection**: This hypothesis imposes Pearl's framework on an empirically derived instrument. Mehling's factor structure was derived from data, not from phenomenological theory. The three-density mapping may be a post-hoc rationalization that fits any 8-subscale instrument if one is creative enough. The second-order three-factor model prediction is testable but may simply fail.

**Unexamined Assumption**: That criterion validity should differ by subscale cluster. Mehling may have found that all eight subscales correlate similarly with all criterion measures, which would not falsify the triadic framework but would suggest the framework is not adding predictive specificity.

### Hypothesis C

**Strongest Support**: Trauma neuroscience data consistently show that interoceptive suppression is a consequence of allostatic load and trauma encoding — not a practice deficit. Somatic therapies produce rapid restoration, not slow skill acquisition. The hormesis principle establishes that regulatory capacity is constitutively present and requires de-inhibition, not construction.

**Strongest Objection**: Dose-dependent improvements with meditation hours are well-documented. Long-term meditators show continued gain on interoceptive measures beyond what ceiling effects would allow if the model were pure restoration. Some individuals show no interoceptive gain despite low allostatic load — suggesting the baseline capacity may vary developmentally in ways the restoration model cannot explain.

**Unexamined Assumption**: That allostatic load and practice hours are adequately separable as predictors in existing studies. Most populations studied in meditation research are also low-allostatic-load populations; confounding may prevent detection of the pattern this hypothesis predicts.

## Synthesis

The three hypotheses are not mutually exclusive. The most parsimonious synthesis:

**Interoceptive awareness operates across three phenomenological registers (detection/regulation/trust) that partially correspond to Pearl's body-soul-spirit triad. Each register has distinct neural substrates (insula-prefrontal connectivity for detection; limbic-prefrontal integration for regulation; default-mode decoupling for trust/listening). The capacity at each register is constitutively present but differentially vulnerable: the body-density register is most vulnerable to allostatic suppression (cortisol, inflammation, sleep disruption); the soul-density register is most vulnerable to attachment disruption and relational trauma; the spirit-density register is most vulnerable to cognitive elaboration and identity crystallization.**

Mindfulness practice (and by extension, Farb's experimental paradigm and MAIA-2's measurement target) operates differentially across registers. Body-density interoceptive practice primarily works through de-inhibition (restoration) of suppressed signal detection. Soul-density practice works through metabolic processing of accumulated relational residue. Spirit-density practice works through attentional training to sustain non-elaborative presence.

This synthesis predicts that MAIA-2's differential criterion validity by subscale cluster (if Mehling 2018 Table 2 shows this) would be a high-value finding — demonstrating that what appears to be one construct (interoceptive awareness) is in fact a family of distinct capacities requiring distinct interventions and distinct criterion measures.
